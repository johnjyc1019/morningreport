## Macro Backdrop

The dominant macro story remains the second wave of inflation sparked by the US-Iran conflict and its stranglehold on global energy supply. April CPI printed at **3.8% YoY** — the hottest reading since May 2023 — with core CPI still running at **+0.4% MoM / +2.8% YoY**, well above the Fed's 2% target. Gasoline surged **28.4%** and fuel oil **54.3%** over the past year, reflecting a near-complete halt to traffic through the Strait of Hormuz now entering its third month of disruption. Oil prices are up roughly **80% year-to-date**, pressing back toward annual highs.

The Fed has held its benchmark rate steady at **3.5%–3.75%** since December, but the rate-cut narrative has definitively collapsed: CME FedWatch now prices the probability of any cut through 2027 at near zero, with more than a **50% chance of a rate hike before year-end 2026**. The US Treasury market confirmed the stress on Friday as the government sold a staggering **$691 billion** in securities during the week, with the **10-year yield spiking to 4.60%** — a fresh one-year high — and the **30-year yield touching 5.12%**, marking the clearest signal yet that bond investors are demanding a premium for duration risk in a re-accelerating inflation regime.

The combined effect of energy-driven inflation, higher-for-longer rates, and fiscal supply pressure creates a challenging backdrop for risk assets as we head into the new week. Duration-sensitive sectors (utilities, REITs, high-multiple tech) face the most direct headwind. Commodities and energy infrastructure remain the structural hedge of choice.

## Trade Ideas

**1. Long Energy Infrastructure (ET, KMI, WMB)**
With Strait of Hormuz disruptions now in their third month and oil up ~80% YTD, North American midstream operators are printing record throughput revenues while benefiting from re-routing of global LNG flows. Midstream's toll-road model insulates from commodity price volatility while capturing volume upside. Pipeline equities also offer a partial duration hedge versus long-duration tech — attractive in a 4.6% 10-year environment. Risk: geopolitical de-escalation compressing energy premia faster than expected.

**2. Long Gold Miners via GDX / Overweight KGC**
Kinross's +7% premarket reaction on a technical miss underscores the market's desire for gold-correlated equity exposure. Real rates remain deeply uncertain as nominal yields rise alongside stubborn inflation; gold miners provide levered upside to spot gold without the futures roll cost. GDX consolidation near support offers a tactical entry. Risk: a sudden ceasefire deal could trigger a sharp unwind of geopolitical risk premia.

**3. Short Duration / Underweight Long-Bond Proxies (Utilities, REITs)**
The 30-year at 5.12% and a Fed that is now leaning toward its next move being a *hike* rather than a cut is a structurally toxic backdrop for yield-proxy equities. Utilities face a dual headwind: rising discount rates compress valuations even as energy cost pass-through timelines lag. A pair trade — long energy infrastructure / short utilities — captures the energy regime shift cleanly. Risk: legislative rate caps or regulated price relief that supports utility earnings.

**4. Tactical Long Astera Labs (ALAB) on AI Infrastructure Theme**
ALAB's 239% EPS beat is not a one-off — it reflects the insatiable demand for high-bandwidth, low-latency interconnect solutions in hyperscale AI clusters. With Nvidia's data-center cycle showing no signs of plateauing and cloud capex guidance from the hyperscalers remaining robust, ALAB sits at a critical enablement layer. The stock's +6% afterhours move is modest relative to the earnings outperformance. Initiate or add on any near-term macro-driven pullback. Risk: customer concentration; any capex guidance cut from major cloud names.
