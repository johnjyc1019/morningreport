## Macro Backdrop

US equity futures are little changed heading into Wednesday's session after a strong end to Q2 on Tuesday. The S&P 500 finished Q2 up ~14%, the Nasdaq soared ~20%, and the Dow added ~12% — a powerful quarter despite persistent inflation headwinds.

The macro backdrop remains complex. Core PCE came in at 3.4% (highest since 2023) and headline PCE at 4.1%, keeping the Fed in wait-and-see mode. Fed Chair Kevin Warsh has re-iterated the central bank's anti-inflation commitment; markets are now pricing a first rate hike as early as September 2026. The 10-year Treasury yield sits around 4.38%, pulling back as oil prices fall sharply — Brent dropped ~4% to ~$73/bbl and WTI is near ~$70/bbl on evidence that Strait of Hormuz tanker flows are normalizing (estimated ~68% of pre-conflict levels restored). Today marks the first trading day of H2 2026.

## POET Technologies (POET) Daily Update

**Current Price:** ~$9.99 (June 30 close) | **Market Cap:** ~$1.7B | **52-Wk Range:** Distorted by April crash and recovery

### Recent News (Last 24 Hours)
No major new press releases or SEC filings detected for July 1, 2026. The stock has stabilized in the $9-$10 range after recovering from the April 27 crash lows. The lead plaintiff deadline in the securities class action (June 29) has now passed — expect formal lead plaintiff appointment proceedings in the coming weeks.

### Key Risk Status

| Risk | Status |
|------|--------|
| **Marvell / Celestial AI order cancellation & NDA lawsuit** | **ACTIVE / UNRESOLVED.** Marvell cancelled all POET purchase orders on April 23, 2026, citing POET's alleged breach of confidentiality obligations. POET's CEO discussed the Celestial AI relationship publicly in a YouTube/Stocktwits interview during a short-seller attack, triggering the NDA termination. Stock fell 47.3% ($7.15/share) on April 27. No settlement disclosed; litigation risk persists. |
| **Securities class actions (PFIC tax misrepresentation)** | **ACTIVE / ESCALATING.** Multiple law firms (Rosen, Levi & Korsinsky, Faruqi & Faruqi, Kirby McInerney, DJS Law Group) filed suits covering the April 1–27, 2026 class period. Allegations: POET misrepresented its PFIC status (using equivocal language that understated near-certain classification), exposed US shareholders to punitive tax treatment, and failed to disclose the NDA breach risk. Lead plaintiff deadline of June 29 has passed — class certification motions likely next. |
| **$400M raise dilution impact** | **KNOWN / ONGOING.** POET closed a registered direct offering on May 18, 2026: 19.05M shares at $21.00 + 19.05M-share warrant (3-year, $26.25 strike). Immediate ~15%+ share count dilution; potential for another ~15% if warrant is exercised. Raise was priced at a premium to the May 14 close ($20.57) but current stock at ~$10 means the institutional buyer is deep underwater. No insider selling disclosed; use of proceeds: manufacturing expansion, R&D, acquisitions. |

### New Positive Catalysts
- **Lumilens partnership:** Supply agreement with initial $50M purchase order; framework potentially worth $500M+ over 5 years — provides some customer diversification away from the now-cancelled Celestial AI concentration
- **Dr. Sandeep Kumar** named COO (effective May 11, 2026) — operational leadership reinforcement
- **Q1 2026 Revenue:** $503K (3x YoY growth from $167K), though absolute figures remain pre-commercial scale

### Outlook: **BEARISH**
Despite the Lumilens deal and manufacturing buildout, POET faces an enormous legal overhang (multiple active class actions now past lead-plaintiff deadline, accelerating into discovery), proven customer-concentration risk (Celestial AI cancellation), and near-zero current revenue relative to a $1.7B market cap. The $400M raise buys time but the institutional backer is ~52% underwater. Until the class actions reach resolution and Lumilens revenue ramps materially, risk/reward skews negative.
