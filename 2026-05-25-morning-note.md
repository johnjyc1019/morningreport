## 1. Macro Backdrop

The macro environment remains the dominant story heading into the second half of May. Inflation refuses to cooperate: April CPI came in at **3.8% YoY** (headline) — the hottest print since May 2023 — while Core CPI held at **2.8% annually** (+0.4% MoM). Energy is the primary culprit, with gasoline +28.4% YoY and fuel oil +54.3% YoY, largely a product of the ongoing **US–Iran conflict** and effective halt of traffic through the **Strait of Hormuz**.

The Federal Reserve's new sheriff, **Kevin Warsh**, officially assumed the Fed Chair role on May 14. His hawkish, rule-based policy philosophy has been well-telegraphed; markets have now priced out rate cuts through end-2027, with a non-trivial probability of an additional **25 bps hike** back on the table by year-end. The **10-year Treasury** last traded at **~4.60%** (a 2026 high), and the **30-year hit 5.1%+**, its highest level in nearly a year. Bond vigilantes are firmly in control.

On geopolitics: US–Iran talks remain stalled. Iran's Supreme Leader over the weekend ordered the country's enriched uranium reserves to remain on Iranian soil, complicating nuclear diplomacy and keeping a premium in energy prices.

**Key rates & commodities (as of Friday, May 22 close):**
- 10-Year UST Yield: **~4.60%**
- 30-Year UST Yield: **~5.10%+**
- Brent Crude: **~$105/bbl**

- Fed Funds Rate: On hold; next move likely a hike, not a cut

## 6. POET Technologies (POET) Daily Update

**Current Price:** ~$14.59 (as of May 22 close) | **Recent range:** $7.97 (post-crash low) → $18.59 (May 14 post-earnings spike)

### Price Action Summary
POET has been extraordinarily volatile over the past month. The stock cratered 47.3% on April 27 following the Celestial AI/Marvell order cancellation, then staged a dramatic 29.4% single-day rebound to $18.59 on May 14 following Q1 2026 earnings and the announced $400M capital raise. By May 22, shares had retraced to $14.59 — down ~21% from the post-earnings spike — reflecting ongoing dilution concerns, litigation overhang, and macro pressure.

### News (Last 24–48 Hours)
- No major new press releases or SEC filings identified over the weekend of May 24–25.
- The $400M direct offering officially **closed May 18** and share issuance is complete.
- New **COO Sandeep Kumar** (effective May 11) continues to ramp into the role; no public comments since appointment.
- Multiple law firms continue to run shareholder outreach campaigns ahead of the **June 29 lead plaintiff deadline**.

### Key Risk Status

| Risk | Status |
|---|---|
| **Marvell/Celestial AI Order Cancellation & NDA Lawsuit** | ⚠️ **Active/Unresolved.** Marvell (post-Celestial AI acquisition) canceled all purchase orders on April 23, 2026 citing POET's CFO disclosing confidential supply chain details publicly. This eliminated a material revenue stream. No settlement reported. NDA breach claim could result in additional civil liability. |
| **Securities Class Actions (PFIC Tax Misrepresentation)** | ⚠️ **Active — Deadline June 29.** Multiple firms (Rosen Law, Levi & Korsinsky, Block & Leviton) allege POET failed to disclose its likely status as a Passive Foreign Investment Company (PFIC) for FY2025, creating adverse US tax consequences for investors. POET has since confirmed PFIC status and offered QEF election guidance. Lead plaintiff window closes **June 29, 2026.** |
| **$400M Raise Dilution Impact** | ⚠️ **Priced In But Ongoing.** Offering closed May 18 at $21.00/share, issuing 19,047,620 new common shares + warrants for 19,047,620 more shares (3-year term). Stock fell 22% on closing day. Full warrant exercise would nearly double current dilution. Proceeds earmarked for manufacturing scale-up, R&D, and potential acquisitions. |

### Brief Outlook: **BEARISH (Near-Term)**

POET faces a toxic trifecta: loss of its flagship customer (Celestial AI/Marvell), mounting securities litigation ahead of a June 29 deadline, and ~22%+ dilution from the May offering with warrant overhang remaining. The $400M provides a capital lifeline, and the long-term optical interposer thesis may still be intact — but until the litigation clears, a new anchor customer is announced, and the stock base stabilizes, risk/reward skews negatively. *Watch for any new commercial design-win announcements as potential catalyst to flip the thesis.*
