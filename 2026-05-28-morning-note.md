## Macro Backdrop

Risk sentiment is cautiously constructive this morning. US equity futures are edging higher after the S&P 500 and Nasdaq closed at fresh record highs on Wednesday, driven by continued AI-sector momentum. The macro backdrop remains complicated: April CPI came in hot at **3.8% YoY** (highest since May 2023), core at **2.8%**, with energy costs surging 17.9% YoY as the US-Iran conflict grinds into its third month and the Strait of Hormuz remains effectively paralyzed. New Fed Chair **Kevin Warsh** (confirmed May 14) is inheriting an environment where rate cuts for 2026 have been virtually priced out. The 10-year Treasury is hovering near **4.60%**, its highest since mid-2025, and the 30-year has recently crossed 5.1%. "Higher for longer" is no longer just a slogan — it is the base case.

## Trade Ideas

1. **Energy longs (XLE / CVX / XOM):** Brent at $96 and heading toward $100 on Hormuz paralysis. As long as the Iran stalemate continues, energy equities remain a macro hedge. Tight risk: a ceasefire headline would reverse this quickly.

2. **AI infrastructure overweight (NVDA / SMCI / ANET):** Despite a muted post-earnings reaction, NVIDIA's data center doubling confirms the capex supercycle is intact. Pullbacks in large-cap AI names remain buyable against 50-DMA support.

3. **Duration short / TBT:** With 10-year yields pressing 4.60% and the Fed on hold indefinitely under Warsh, there is still room for yields to grind higher. TBT (2x inverse 20-yr Treasury) is a tactical expression with defined risk.

4. **PayPal (PYPL) dip watch:** The 9% premarket flush looks punitive for a company that beat on both lines. If the sell-off holds into the open, PYPL near its 200-DMA could offer a mean-reversion setup for active traders — though guidance language needs scrutiny at open.

## POET Technologies (POET) Daily Update

**Price (May 27 close):** $13.28 | **1-Month:** +72% | **Since May 14 peak:** -28%

### News (Last 24 Hours)
No new press releases or SEC filings detected in the last 24 hours. The stock continues to trade in the aftermath of several overlapping catalysts from the past month.

### Key Risk Status

| Risk | Status |
|---|---|
| **(1) Marvell/Celestial AI cancellation & NDA lawsuit** | **Active / Unresolved.** On April 27, Marvell (post-Celestial AI acquisition) cancelled all POET purchase orders, citing confidentiality violations — POET allegedly disclosed order/shipping details in breach of NDA. Shares fell 47.3% in a single session. No settlement or resolution announced. NDA breach exposes POET to potential civil liability beyond the lost revenue. |
| **(2) Securities class action (PFIC tax misrepresentation)** | **Active.** Multiple firms (Rosen Law, Block & Leviton, Levi & Korsinsky) have filed or are investigating. Class period: April 1–April 27, 2026. Allegation: POET concealed likely PFIC status under US tax law, exposing US shareholders to complex tax liabilities. Lead plaintiff deadline: **June 29, 2026.** POET confirmed PFIC status for FY2025 on April 14 and announced plans to redomicile to the US. |
| **(3) $400M raise dilution** | **Closed / Overhang persists.** POET closed a $400M registered direct offering on May 18, 2026 at $21.00/share (a slight premium to the May 14 close of $20.57) — issuing 19,047,620 new shares plus an equal number of warrants exercisable over 3 years. Full exercise would double the dilution. Stock fell 22% on the close date. Triggered by a Lumilens JDA with a $50M initial purchase order. Capital earmarked for manufacturing scale-up, R&D, and acquisitions. |

### Outlook: **BEARISH (near-term)**
With three concurrent overhangs — litigation, dilution, and the loss of its most significant revenue pipeline — POET faces an unusually heavy risk-reward burden; until the class action reaches a resolution or a credible replacement customer pipeline is disclosed, the path of least resistance remains lower.
