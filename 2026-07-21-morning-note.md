## POET Technologies (POET) Daily Update

**Current Price:** ~$7.54 (July 20, 2026 close) | **Daily Range:** $7.46–$7.74 | **52-Week Range:** $3.87–$20.81

**Recent Performance:** POET is down roughly 64% from its May 2026 registered direct offering price of $21/share. As of July 20, the stock closed at approximately $7.54, recovering modestly from June lows but still deeply depressed versus the capital raise level.

### News (Last 24 Hours)
No material new press releases or SEC filings detected in the past 24 hours as of this writing. The stock appears to be trading on residual sentiment and no new catalysts.

### Key Risk Status

**(1) Marvell/Celestial AI Order Cancellation & NDA Lawsuit**
- **Status: Active / Unresolved.** Marvell Technology (which acquired Celestial AI in February 2026) issued written notice on April 23, 2026, canceling all outstanding POET purchase orders, citing breach of confidentiality obligations — POET allegedly disclosed Celestial AI order details publicly after the Marvell acquisition. POET stock fell 46–47% on the disclosure. No formal litigation outcome has been reported; the NDA dispute and potential counterclaims remain an overhang.

**(2) Securities Class Actions (including PFIC Tax Misrepresentation)**
- **Status: Active / Post-Deadline.** Multiple law firms (Kirby McInerney, Faruqi & Faruqi, DJS Law Group, SueWallSt) filed securities class action lawsuits covering the April 1–27, 2026 class period. Key allegations: (a) POET made false/misleading statements about its PFIC (Passive Foreign Investment Company) tax classification, using equivocal language ("may be treated" as PFIC) while the risk was allegedly more concrete; (b) CFO Thomas Mika's public interview constituted breach of the business agreement with Marvell. Lead plaintiff deadline passed June 29, 2026 — cases are now in active litigation.

**(3) $400M Capital Raise — Dilution Impact**
- **Status: Dilutive / Poorly Timed.** POET completed a registered direct offering of 19,047,620 common shares plus warrants at $21.00/unit in May 2026, raising $400M gross. The stock has since fallen ~64% below the offering price, meaning investors in that raise are deeply underwater. Proceeds are earmarked for manufacturing scale-up, M&A, R&D, and working capital. Balance sheet is bolstered, but dilution pressure (~19M new shares) and negative share price momentum create headwinds.

### Brief Outlook: **BEARISH**
Stock remains under severe pressure: class actions in active litigation, the NDA dispute with Marvell unresolved, revenue still tiny ($503K in Q1 2026), and the May capital raise deeply underwater. Without a credible new customer announcement or legal resolution, the path of least resistance is sideways-to-down. Any Marvell settlement or new strategic partnership could act as a catalyst, but there is no near-term catalyst visible.
