## Macro Backdrop

US equities pushed to fresh all-time highs last week as the Q2 earnings season kicks off in earnest. The primary headwinds remain the US–Iran conflict around the Strait of Hormuz (driving oil prices sharply higher) and persistent above-target inflation keeping the Fed on hold. The 10-year Treasury eased to ~4.54% Friday after two consecutive sessions of declines, aided by a partial giveback in crude following signals from the White House of renewed diplomatic outreach with Tehran. The Fed voted 12–0 to hold the federal funds target at 3.50–3.75% and dropped its easing bias at its last meeting; median end-2026 dot was lifted to 3.8% from 3.4%, with officials citing tariffs, supply-chain disruption, and AI-driven investment as forces keeping inflation above 2%.

### News in Last 24 Hours
- No new SEC filings or press releases detected for July 12–13, 2026 at time of publication.
- Company AGM results filed (June 26, 2026): all director nominees re-elected; management reiterated production ramp on schedule for H2 2026, targeting up to 1 million optical engine units/month by end of 2027.
- Total equity raised now stands at ~$830M over the past 12 months, with up to ~$661M more potentially available via outstanding warrants.

### Key Risk Status

| Risk | Status |
|---|---|
| **(1) Marvell/Celestial AI order cancellation & NDA lawsuit** | **Active / Unresolved.** On April 23, 2026, Marvell notified POET of cancellation of all Celestial AI purchase orders, citing a confidentiality breach by POET's CEO (YouTube interview, April 21, 2026). No settlement or resolution announced. NDA breach is both a direct revenue hit and the triggering event for class actions. |
| **(2) Securities class actions (PFIC tax misrepresentation)** | **Active / Post-deadline.** Lead plaintiff deadline was June 29, 2026 (Faruqi & Faruqi, Levi & Korsinsky, Kirby McInerney, Rosen Law, Block & Leviton all filed). Cases allege POET misrepresented its Passive Foreign Investment Company (PFIC) status, carrying adverse US tax consequences for shareholders. Litigation now in consolidation/lead-plaintiff phase — resolution likely 12–24 months out. |
| **(3) $400M raise dilution impact** | **Permanent / Ongoing.** Registered direct offering closed ~May 18, 2026: 19,047,620 shares at $21.00 plus a warrant for an equal number of shares (exercise price $26.25, 3-year term). Stock fell 22%+ on closing day. At current price (~$8.50), shares trade far below both offering and warrant prices — warrants are deeply out of the money. Full dilution if warrants ever exercise: ~38M new shares on a base that makes the raise look even more punishing retrospectively. |

### Outlook: **Neutral / Cautiously Bearish**
POET's production ramp narrative remains intact and the technology platform is credible, but the stock faces a severe overhang: multiple ongoing securities lawsuits, loss of its most visible customer relationship, and shares trading at ~60% below the May capital raise price. Until the class action litigation provides clarity and/or a replacement anchor customer is announced, risk/reward is unfavorable for new longs. Watch for any partnership announcements or design wins to change the thesis.
