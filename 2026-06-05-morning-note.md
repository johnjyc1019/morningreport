## Macro Backdrop

US equity futures are lower Friday morning as markets brace for the May employment report, the most consequential data point ahead of the June 16–17 FOMC meeting. The macro backdrop remains unfriendly: April CPI printed 3.8% YoY — the highest since 2023 and nearly double the Fed's 2% target — keeping rate-hike bets elevated (markets now price an 85% probability of a quarter-point hike by year-end). The Fed funds target rate sits at 3.50–3.75%. The 10-year Treasury yield climbed to 4.49% mid-week, with risk of testing the 4.5%+ threshold if oil stays elevated. Brent crude remains above $100/bbl, underpinned by Middle East risk premium as the US–Iran ceasefire holds but nuclear negotiations remain stalled. The Fed and ECB have both signaled a cautious tone on Q3 rate adjustments. Separately, the first phase of a global AI Governance Framework began implementation this week, mandating transparency disclosures (training data sources, safety audits) for large-scale generative models within 180 days.

## Trade Ideas

1. **HPE — Tactical long.** The 30% premarket gap on a near-doubling of full-year EPS guidance reflects genuine AI server demand. Near-term momentum favors longs on any intraday fade below the gap open. Stop below prior-day close.

2. **PANW — Hold / add on dips.** Consistent execution, strong platformization narrative, and raised guidance support a core position. AI security spending looks secular. The macro headwind (risk-off into jobs data) could offer a better entry today.

3. **Energy (XLE/CVX/XOM) — Maintain long bias.** Brent >$100, stalled Iran negotiations, and sticky inflation create a constructive backdrop. A weak jobs print could briefly pressure, but the structural supply story remains intact.

4. **TLT (20-yr Treasury ETF) — Tactical short into FOMC.** With CPI at 3.8% and the market pricing a hike, the risk-reward for duration longs is poor ahead of June 16–17. Short-dated Treasuries are more attractive for cash management.

5. **POET — Avoid / hold off.** Extraordinary legal and dilution overhang (see below) makes this untradeable for most investors near-term. Speculative longs should wait for class action resolution and AGM clarity.

| Risk | Status |
|---|---|
| **(1) Marvell / Celestial AI order cancellation & NDA lawsuit** | **Active / Unresolved.** On April 27, Marvell cancelled all Celestial AI purchase orders after POET's CFO allegedly disclosed confidential PO details in an April 21 social media interview. ### Outlook: **BEARISH (Near-Term)**
The loss of Marvell/Celestial AI as a revenue driver, cascading securities litigation with a June 29 deadline, and a heavily dilutive $400M equity raise with an institutional warrant overhang combine to create a deeply uncertain risk/reward profile. Without a new named customer or partnership announcement, the stock lacks a positive re-rating catalyst. The June 26 AGM is the next scheduled event where management could address these issues directly — any progress on NDA resolution or new customer disclosure would be the key upside trigger to watch.
