## Macro Backdrop

June CPI came in sharply below expectations — headline +3.5% YoY (-0.4% MoM) vs. est. +3.8%, core +2.6% YoY — triggering a Treasury rally and a reset in Fed pricing. The 10-year yield sits at ~4.555% (-3bps overnight), and odds of a July hike have collapsed to ~17%. The market now assigns ~60% probability to a September hike, keeping the Fed on pause for now. The Fed funds rate remains at 3.50%–3.75% (held since June 2026).

Geopolitically, the US-Iran ceasefire has broken down, with both sides exchanging strikes overnight and the US reinstating a naval blockade near the Strait of Hormuz. Brent crude has climbed above $85/bbl — up ~16% from its recent low — creating a renewed inflationary tail risk that complicates the Fed's path and weighs on transport and consumer discretionary.

China's Q2 GDP came in at +4.3% YoY, the weakest print since 2022 and below the government's official target range, adding pressure to global demand expectations.

1. **Long TSMC (TSM)** ahead of print — AI infrastructure capex is accelerating and TSMC is the single-source beneficiary of next-gen packaging. Options market pricing a ~$150B market cap swing; favor a binary long via calls or shares with a tight stop below $200.

2. **Long GE Aerospace (GE)** — commercial aviation demand continues to outstrip supply; aftermarket revenue (spare engines, services) offers high-margin recurring stream. Report this morning; consensus appears conservative.

3. **Cautious on UnitedHealth (UNH)** — elevated medical cost trends in Medicare Advantage remain a live overhang. Wait for clarity on the medical loss ratio before adding. Sector could move sharply on guidance.

4. **Energy longs / inflation hedges** — US-Iran escalation and Strait of Hormuz blockade justify a tactical long in oil producers (e.g., XOM, CVX) or a long Brent futures position. The inflationary feedback loop also supports short-duration Treasuries.

5. **Watch ASML (ASML)** — guidance raised to €43–45B; the stock gained ~3% yesterday. European semis may continue to outperform on AI CapEx spillover.

## POET Technologies (POET) Daily Update

**Price: $9.56 | Daily Change: -5.91%**
*(Previous close: $10.16 | Intraday range: $9.31–$9.97 | Market cap: ~$1.65B | Volume: $22.24M)*

### News (Last 24 Hours)
No material SEC filings or press releases in the past 24 hours. The most recent positive coverage was a 24/7 Wall St. bull thesis published July 14, setting a price target of **$22.23** (~163% upside) contingent on the H2 2026 Malaysia manufacturing ramp for 800G optical engines and Lumilens light source shipments executing on schedule.

### Key Risk Status

| Risk | Status |
|---|---|
| **Marvell/Celestial AI order cancellation & NDA lawsuit** | Active / Unresolved. Marvell cancelled all Celestial AI POs on April 23, 2026 (46% single-day drop), citing POET's alleged NDA breaches by board member Thomas Mika. No settlement announced. This eliminates POET's largest disclosed revenue stream. |
| **Securities class actions (PFIC tax misrepresentation)** | Active. Three class-action suits filed; class period April 1–April 27, 2026. Allege POET misrepresented PFIC classification risk to US shareholders and that the NDA breach by Mika triggered the Celestial AI cancellation. Lead plaintiff deadline passed June 29. Litigation risk ongoing. |
| **$400M capital raise dilution** | Severe overhang. Raise closed May 18 at $21.00/share + warrant. Stock now trading at $9.56 — a ~55% discount to the raise price, implying significant underwater dilution for the May buyers and persistent selling pressure. |

### Positives
- **Lumilens agreement**: $50M initial purchase order; potential to scale to $500M+ over five years.
- **Production ramp**: Guidance for 30,000+ optical engines in 2026; high-volume light source production in Q2, 800G engines in Q3 from Malaysia facility.
- **Institutional accumulation**: Citadel disclosed a 5.1–5.9% stake; Jane Street 6.8% — institutional investors appear to be buying into the drawdown.
- **YTD performance**: Still +26% YTD despite the post-April selloff.

### Outlook: **Bearish / Neutral**
POET's fundamental photonics technology story remains intact, but the near-term investment case is impaired. The stock is down ~52% from its May peak and trades well below the $400M raise price, with three unresolved lawsuits, the loss of its largest customer, and no clear near-term revenue catalyst to replace Celestial AI. The Lumilens deal is encouraging but unproven. For patient, risk-tolerant investors, the H2 Malaysia ramp is the sole catalyst worth watching. **Avoid new long positions until litigation clarity improves or Lumilens shipments are confirmed.**
