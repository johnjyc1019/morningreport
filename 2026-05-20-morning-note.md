## Macro Backdrop

The dominant macro theme remains the US-Iran conflict, now entering its third month. Overnight headlines reflect continued uncertainty after President Trump characterized his pause on military action as "only temporary," unwinding about half of Tuesday's late-session S&P rally. The bond market is the key pressure point: the 10-year Treasury yield touched a 16-month high of **4.70%** and the 30-year surged to an 18-year high of **5.20%**, reflecting a second wave of inflation triggered largely by an energy shock. April CPI printed at **3.8% YoY** with core at **2.8% YoY**; energy costs rose 17.9% YoY, with gasoline +28.4% and fuel oil +54.3%. The Fed (funds rate: 3.5%–3.75%) is widely expected to hold through year-end, though implied probability of a 25 bps hike has climbed to ~40%. Brent crude is at **$112.10/bbl** (+2.6%) and WTI at **$108.66/bbl** (+3.1%), up ~60% since the Iran conflict began.

The S&P 500 has recouped its entire 9% pullback and eclipsed fresh all-time highs on strong Q1 earnings (84% beat rate, +12.3% avg. magnitude vs. 5-yr avg. of 7.3%), but the overnight drift lower in futures signals the geopolitical risk premium is not going away. Key scheduled event today: **FOMC minutes** (2 PM ET).

## Trade Ideas

1. **Short duration fixed income / long TIPS** — 10-year at 4.70% and 30-year at 5.20% reflect a genuine repricing of the inflation risk premium, not a technical overshoot. If FOMC minutes today lean hawkish, the move has more room.

2. **Energy long (XLE / Brent)** — Strait of Hormuz disruptions are structural, not a spike. Oil is up 60% YTD on the conflict; until a ceasefire framework materializes, energy equities have fundamental support.

3. **Defensive rotation (XLU / XLV)** — With mega-cap tech (Meta −9%, MSFT −4%) repricing capex concerns and Iran uncertainty weighing on risk appetite, utilities and healthcare offer lower duration and geopolitical insulation.

4. **Nvidia event risk** — With Meta and MSFT selling off on heavy capex guidance, Nvidia's own print is a two-sided catalyst. A downward revision to hyperscaler AI spend could drag the AI semis complex sharply lower. Consider hedging via short-dated puts ahead of the print.

## POET Technologies (POET) Daily Update

**Current Price:** ~$13.21 (intraday range: $11.82 – $13.92)
**Daily Change:** Volatile; stock has shed ~22% since the May 18 capital raise closing; up ~207% over the past month and ~225% YTD despite recent turbulence.

### News Last 24 Hours
No new SEC filings or press releases identified as of publication. The $400M direct offering formally closed May 18, 2026. New COO Sandeep Kumar was appointed May 11. The Lumilens supply agreement (EOI optical engines, large initial order + multi-year framework) remains the primary near-term revenue narrative.

### Key Risk Status

| Risk | Status |
|---|---|
| **Marvell/Celestial AI order cancellation & NDA lawsuit** | **Active / Unresolved.** On April 27, Marvell (which acquired Celestial AI) cancelled all purchase orders, citing POET's CFO disclosing confidential shipment and invoice data in a public interview on April 21. POET shares fell 47.3% on the announcement. No settlement or legal resolution disclosed. |
| **Securities class actions (PFIC misrepresentation)** | **Active.** Multiple firms (Rosen, Faruqi, Levi & Korsinsky, RGRD Law) filed class actions alleging POET misrepresented its PFIC tax status (Wolfpack Research short report, April 14, triggered an 8% decline) and that executives made misleading statements about NDA-covered customer relationships. Class period: April 1 – April 27, 2026. Lead plaintiff deadline: **June 29, 2026.** |
| **$400M raise dilution impact** | **Material and ongoing.** 19,047,620 new shares issued at $21.00 plus a 3-year warrant for an equal number of shares at the same price. An additional 22.9M warrants were granted to Lumilens at $8.25 (9-year term). Potential fully-diluted share count increase is substantial. Stock dropped 22% on the offering close. |

### Outlook: **Neutral/Bearish with high uncertainty**
The Lumilens agreement and $400M war chest provide a credible manufacturing scale-up narrative, but the overhang from multiple active lawsuits, the Marvell relationship effectively severed, and heavy dilution suppress near-term upside; the stock needs legal clarity before a durable re-rating.
