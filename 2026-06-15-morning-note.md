# Morning Note — June 15, 2026

---

## Macro Backdrop

The dominant story overnight is a geopolitical inflection: the US and Iran reached a deal to reopen the Strait of Hormuz, triggering a broad risk-on surge across global equities, a sharp drop in crude, and a flight back into risk assets. Brent crude tumbled more than 4% toward $83/barrel — a meaningful reversal from levels above $100 that had persisted amid Strait-closure fears. The dollar weakened against major peers and Bitcoin hit a two-week high.

The macro backdrop into the week is complicated. May CPI printed at 4.2% YoY — the hottest inflation since 2023 — driven largely by a 23.5% annual surge in energy prices. Core inflation came in at +0.2% MoM, softer than the +0.3% consensus. The Fed holds its next FOMC meeting on June 17 and is universally expected to stand pat at 3.50%–3.75%. However, the CME FedWatch Tool now prices a ~70% probability of at least one rate hike before year-end, keeping the long end under pressure. The 10-year Treasury yield edged down to 4.53%, catching a modest geopolitical bid as the Iran resolution eased some stagflationary energy pressure.

## POET Technologies (POET) Daily Update

**Current Price:** $12.71 (June 14 close; +12.8% on June 12 following $400M raise announcement)

### Last 24-Hour News
- No material new filings or press releases detected overnight as of June 15 morning.
- Stock remains in the $10.99–$12.93 range established June 14, well below the $21.00 direct-offering price from the May capital raise.

### Key Risk Status

**(1) Marvell/Celestial AI Order Cancellation & NDA Lawsuit**
On April 23, 2026, Marvell Semiconductor (which acquired Celestial AI) notified POET of the cancellation of all purchase orders. The stated basis: POET's CEO appeared in a video interview on April 21 — following a short-seller report — and disclosed confidential details about Celestial AI invoices and shipping timelines, breaching the NDA. Stock fell 47.3% on April 27. Status: **Active risk — no settlement or resolution reported. NDA litigation exposure remains open.**

**(2) Securities Class Actions (PFIC Tax Misrepresentation)**
Multiple law firms (Rosen Law, Pomerantz, Faruqi & Faruqi, Block & Leviton) have filed or are investigating class actions on behalf of shareholders who purchased POET stock between April 1–27, 2026. Core allegation: POET's 2025 Annual Report materially understated the likelihood of PFIC (Passive Foreign Investment Company) classification under US tax law — which carries significantly adverse tax consequences for US investors — using equivocal language ("may be treated") to obscure a more certain negative outcome. **Lead plaintiff deadline: June 29, 2026.** Status: **Imminent milestone — elevated legal headline risk this week.**

**(3) $400M Raise — Dilution Impact**
POET closed a non-brokered registered direct offering of 19,047,620 shares at $21.00/share plus three-year warrants exercisable at $26.15, raising ~$400M gross. Proceeds target ~10x manufacturing capacity expansion, R&D scale-up, accelerated light-source development, potential M&A, and working capital. At current ~$12.71, shares trade at a 39% discount to the offering price, and the 19M+ new shares represent substantial dilution. Warrants at $26.15 represent a distant 2x+ upside before creating further overhang. Status: **Near-term dilutive — warrants add secondary overhang; stock must more than double from here to trigger warrant exercise pressure.**

### Outlook: **Cautiously Bearish / Speculative**
The combination of an active NDA/cancellation dispute with Marvell, multiple securities class actions with a June 29 lead-plaintiff deadline, and shares trading 39% below the recent capital raise price reflects a stock under significant fundamental and legal pressure. The $400M raise provides a runway narrative, and Lumilens ($50M order), LITEON, and Lessengers collaborations are positive pipeline signals — but without the Celestial AI revenue anchor and with PFIC tax clarity still unresolved, near-term catalysts are limited. Risk/reward is asymmetric to the downside unless the NDA dispute is settled or a new anchor customer is announced.
