## Macro Backdrop

Risk-on tone persists heading into Friday's open, led by a semiconductor and AI rebound that's outrunning fresh geopolitical anxieties in the Middle East. U.S.-Iran military exchanges near the Strait of Hormuz have closed the waterway — which handles roughly 20% of global oil and gas shipments — sending energy names higher and capping the broader rally. The Fed remains on hold at 3.50–3.75%, but the June dot plot lifted the median end-2026 rate to 3.8%, and markets are now pricing a ~64% probability of a hike at the September meeting. The 10-year Treasury eased slightly to ~4.54% on falling oil-price concerns; a second leg lower in yields would open room for equities to run harder. Next major catalyst: June CPI on July 14.

## Trade Ideas

1. **Long Semiconductor ETF (SOXX) / NVDA, AVGO** — AI capital expenditure cycle is intact; SK Hynix's U.S. debut and ARM's results reinforce demand. Strait of Hormuz risk is a near-term overhang but not a semiconductor-specific headwind. Entry on any intraday dip.

2. **Long Energy (XLE / XOM)** — Strait of Hormuz closure is a genuine supply shock. Even partial resolution leaves a geopolitical premium in crude. Energy equities haven't fully priced the move in Brent. Tight stop below pre-event WTI (~$68).

3. **Avoid / Underweight PEP** — Margin deterioration in North America beverages, guidance toward low end, and broader consumer spending caution argue for staying sidelined. The miss was worse than the number implies; watch for further analyst cuts next week.

4. **POET Technologies (POET) — Speculative Watch Only** — Not a clean long. Multiple litigation overhangs and dilution risk from the $400M raise limit near-term upside. Monitor for any news on lawsuit resolution or production ramp confirmation before adding exposure.

## POET Technologies (POET) Daily Update

**Current Price:** ~$8.76 (as of July 9, 2026 close, +3.06% on the day)
**52-Week Range:** $3.87 – $20.81

### Last 24-Hour News
No major new SEC filings or press releases confirmed for July 9–10. Stock is recovering gradually from April's crash lows. The Benzinga technical note highlights that shares are defending the 200-day moving average — a minor positive signal in an otherwise challenged chart. Total equity raised now stands at ~$830M over the past 12 months.

### Key Risk Status

| Risk | Status |
|---|---|
| **Marvell / Celestial AI Order Cancellation & NDA Lawsuit** | ACTIVE — POET EVP disclosed confidential order/shipping details in a April 21 YouTube/Stocktwits interview, triggering Marvell's cancellation of all purchase orders on April 27. Stock fell 47.3% that day ($15.10 → $7.95). NDA breach is the operative legal and commercial wound; no settlement disclosed. |
| **Securities Class Actions (PFIC Tax Misrepresentation)** | ACTIVE — Multiple law firms (Rosen, Levi & Korsinsky, Faruqi & Faruqi, Kirby McInerney) filed actions in NJ District Court. Class period: April 1–27, 2026. Core allegation: POET obscured near-certain PFIC classification using equivocal annual-report language. Lead plaintiff deadline of June 29 has passed; litigation moves to discovery phase. No settlement or dismissal motion yet. |
| **$400M Capital Raise Dilution** | CLOSED / ONGOING RISK — Offering closed May 2026 at $21.00/share (slight premium at the time). 19,047,620 new shares issued + 19,047,620 warrant shares potential. Stock dropped 22% on announcement. Dilution is now permanent; additional warrant exercise risk remains for 3 years. Proceeds earmarked for manufacturing scale-up and capex (~$50M H2 2026). |

### Outlook: NEUTRAL / CAUTIOUSLY BEARISH

Litigation is in active phase with no resolution in sight, and the dilution from the May raise is baked into the float. The production ramp narrative (optical engines targeting 1M units/month by end-2027) is the bull case, but execution risk is high and the company has burned credibility with its largest customer. Any catalyst from lawsuit settlement talks or a new commercial win could shift the tone quickly — but absent that, this is dead money with asymmetric downside risk to litigation outcomes.
