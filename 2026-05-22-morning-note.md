## Macro Backdrop

US equity futures are pointing modestly higher ahead of the open, with S&P 500 futures up ~0.32% to 7,490 and Nasdaq futures up ~0.41% to 29,567. The macro setup remains complicated by sticky inflation and a divided Fed.

April CPI came in at **3.8% YoY** (highest since May 2023), with Core CPI at +2.8% YoY — still well above the Fed's 2% target. Energy costs were the primary driver, surging 17.9% YoY on the back of US-Iran tensions that have effectively stalled Strait of Hormuz traffic since late Q1. The Fed held the funds rate unchanged at **3.50%–3.75%** at its most recent meeting, but the decision drew the largest internal dissent in over 30 years (8-4 vote). CME FedWatch now prices in near-zero probability of rate cuts through end of 2027. The **10-year Treasury yield** sits at ~**4.46%**, approaching its 2026 high of 4.48%. Crude remains elevated but has pulled back from mid-month highs, with WTI at ~**$96.35/bbl** and Brent at ~**$102.58/bbl** after falling ~2% yesterday on tentative US-Iran ceasefire diplomacy signals.

The S&P 500 has fully recovered its 9% geopolitical-driven drawdown from Q1 and is trading near all-time highs. However, the reward for earnings beats has been unusually muted — median S&P 500 beat is outperforming the index by just +20 bps the day after reporting, one of the smallest readings on record, suggesting positioning into earnings is rich.

The stock's V-shaped recovery from $8 to $21 in three weeks reflects speculative positioning more than fundamental clarity. Hold existing positions cautiously; new entries warrant tight risk management given litigation uncertainty and warrant dilution ceiling.
