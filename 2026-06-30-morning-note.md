## Macro Backdrop

Risk sentiment is modestly positive to start the final trading day of Q2 2026, lifted by a weekend breakthrough: Washington and Tehran have agreed to suspend further military action and resume diplomatic talks, pushing oil sharply lower and partially unwinding the Middle East risk premium that had dominated markets through the quarter.

The Fed remains on hold at **3.50%–3.75%** (fourth consecutive pause, June meeting), while the latest PCE print—**4.1% headline / 3.4% core**—sits well above target and gives the FOMC no room to ease. The dot plot showed nine officials see at least one hike still warranted this year. The 10-year Treasury ended last week at **4.39%**, roughly flat on the week after the in-line inflation report.

Oil's decline is the standout macro development: **Brent crude fell ~4% to ~$73/bbl** and **WTI to ~$70/bbl**, reaching pre-Iran-war levels. This provides a meaningful disinflationary impulse at the margin but does not resolve structurally sticky services inflation. Energy-importing regions (Europe, parts of EM) are the primary beneficiaries.

## Trade Ideas

1. **Long energy importers / short energy producers** — US/Iran ceasefire has reset oil to pre-war levels. If talks hold, the disinflationary impulse benefits airlines, transports, and consumer discretionary more than it hurts domestic energy names (already partially priced).

2. **Short-duration Treasuries / tactical TIPS** — With core PCE at 3.4% and the Fed on hold-to-hike, the front end offers attractive real yields. TIPS breakevens may lag the oil move as services inflation remains sticky.

3. **Defensive rotation into Japan exporters** — Nikkei's +37.8% YTD lead reflects yen weakness and corporate governance reform. A ceasefire-driven risk-on pulse + still-weak yen keeps the trade intact into H2.

4. **Avoid speculative small-cap photonics / semiconductors** — POET Technologies is a live cautionary tale: overleveraged narrative, customer concentration, legal overhang, and massive dilution (see POET section below).

### Recent News (Last 24 Hours)

### Key Risk Status

| Risk | Status |
|---|---|
| **(1) Marvell/Celestial AI cancellation & NDA lawsuit** | **Active.** Marvell (via Celestial AI acquisition) canceled all purchase orders April 23, citing an executive's YouTube video that allegedly disclosed confidential shipment/invoice details. Stock fell 47.3% on April 27. Securities fraud complaints filed April 28 in D.N.J. allege executives made materially false statements during the April 1–27 class period. Litigation ongoing; no settlement or dismissal. |
| **(2) Securities class actions (PFIC tax misrepresentation)** | **Active / Escalating.** Lead plaintiff deadline was June 29; the litigation now moves to court. Allegations: POET's 2025 Annual Report used equivocal language ("may be treated") to understate the likelihood of PFIC classification under U.S. tax law, carrying negative tax consequences for U.S. shareholders. Combined with the NDA breach allegation, the lawsuit asserts a pattern of material misrepresentation. Discovery expected to take months. |
| **(3) $400M raise dilution impact** | **Significant / Ongoing.** On May 15, POET closed a registered direct offering at $21.00/share: 19.05M new shares issued, plus a 3-year warrant for an additional 19.05M shares at $26.25. The offering caused a 22% single-day drop and the 10-year CFO resigned. At a current price of ~$9.42 vs. the $21.00 offering price, existing investors who participated are deeply underwater. Full warrant exercise would nearly double the dilution. Proceeds earmarked for manufacturing expansion and M&A. |

### Brief Outlook: **BEARISH**
The stock is down ~55% from its $21.00 May offering price and ~56% from its 52-week high, with three compounding headwinds—customer concentration loss (Marvell/Celestial AI being the flagship order), active multi-firm securities litigation now entering the lead-plaintiff phase, and massive share dilution with the CFO who managed the balance sheet now gone. No near-term catalysts are visible before the August 19 earnings report, which will be the first opportunity to address investor concerns. Avoid until legal clarity emerges or a credible customer replacement is announced.
