## POET Technologies (POET) Daily Update

**Price & Change:** Live intraday data unavailable at time of writing. Contextual reference points: stock fell ~47.3% on April 27 (order cancellation shock); $400M equity offering priced at $21.00/share on May 18; headlines as recently as July 2 describe the stock continuing to reel under mounting class action pressure.

**News (Last 24 Hours):** No new press releases or SEC filings identified for July 6. The most recent filing is the **June 30, 2026 Form 6-K** (annual meeting recap / CEO commercial update).

**Key Risk Status**

| Risk | Status | Summary |
|------|--------|---------|
| (1) Marvell / Celestial AI Order Cancellation & NDA Lawsuit | **ACTIVE — High** | Celestial AI cancelled all purchase orders in April 2026 after a POET executive publicly disclosed confidential customer terms, breaching the NDA. The stock fell 47.3% ($7.15/share) on corrective disclosure. Commercial damage and civil NDA liability exposure remain unresolved. |
| (2) Securities Class Actions (PFIC Tax Misrepresentation) | **ACTIVE — Lead Plaintiff Filed** | Multiple law firms (Faruqi & Faruqi, Levi & Korsinsky, SueWallSt) filed class actions covering the April 1–27, 2026 class period. Lead plaintiff deadline was June 29, 2026 — the litigation is now in active development. Core allegation: POET's 2025 Annual Report used equivocal language ("may be treated as a PFIC") that materially understated the probability of the adverse PFIC tax classification for U.S. shareholders. |
| (3) $400M Raise Dilution Impact | **CLOSED — Ongoing Overhang** | On May 18, 2026, POET closed a registered direct offering: 19,047,620 shares at $21.00 + warrants for an additional 19,047,620 shares (exercise price $26.25, 3-year term). Net proceeds directed toward manufacturing scale-up and R&D acceleration. With ~38M potential shares outstanding, dilution is a material headwind to near-term price recovery. |

**Brief Outlook: Bearish.** POET is navigating three concurrent crises — the loss of its most publicly disclosed customer (Celestial AI/Marvell), active securities fraud litigation that cleared the lead plaintiff threshold on June 29, and ~38M-share dilution from the May raise. The June 30 annual meeting update (>10 customer engagements, $830M raised over 12 months, H2 2026 production ramp target) offers a constructive long-term narrative, but near-term price action is dominated by litigation headline risk and share overhang. **Remain on the sidelines; revisit after any court filings or new customer revenue disclosure.**
