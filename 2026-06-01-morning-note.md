### Key Risk Tracker

**1. Marvell / Celestial AI Order Cancellation & NDA Lawsuit**
> **Status: ACTIVE / HIGH RISK**
> POET alleged that Marvell Technology and/or Celestial AI cancelled a material product order and may have misappropriated confidential information in breach of NDA. This is the company's most operationally significant risk: if the cancellation stands, it removes a key near-term revenue assumption that underpinned the bull case for their optical engine ramp. The NDA lawsuit outcome is binary — a favorable settlement or win could validate POET's IP position; a loss or dismissal with no recovery leaves the company with a revenue hole and damaged customer credibility. **Monitor for any court filings or settlement disclosures.**

**2. Securities Class Action Lawsuits — PFIC Tax Misrepresentation**
> **Status: ACTIVE / ELEVATED RISK**
> One or more securities class actions have been filed alleging that POET failed to adequately disclose its status as a Passive Foreign Investment Company (PFIC) under U.S. tax law, which carries punitive tax treatment for U.S. shareholders. As a Canadian-incorporated company listed on NASDAQ, POET is subject to PFIC rules if it fails to generate sufficient active business income. Class actions at this stage typically depress the stock and raise the cost of future equity issuances. Key question: does POET have viable PFIC Annual Information Statements (PAIS) that could have informed U.S. investors? **Watch for lead plaintiff appointment and motion to dismiss timeline.**

**3. $400M Capital Raise — Dilution Impact**
> **Status: MATERIAL OVERHANG**
> A $400M capital raise is extremely large relative to POET's historical market capitalization (which has generally been in the $100M–$500M range depending on sentiment). Depending on the structure (common equity, convertible notes, warrants, ATM offering), this raise likely implies severe dilution to existing shareholders — potentially 50–200%+ share count expansion at depressed prices. Key questions: (a) What was the price/terms of the raise? (b) Has the capital been deployed? (c) Does it buy enough runway to reach product revenue milestones? Until the dilution overhang is fully absorbed and the company shows it can convert cash into revenue, this is a structural headwind. **Monitor for S-3 shelf registration amendments and prospectus supplements.**

---

### Outlook: **BEARISH / HIGH RISK**

POET faces a compound risk stack that is difficult to overcome in the near term: a cancelled anchor customer order that guts the near-term revenue thesis, active class action litigation that limits institutional participation, and a dilutive capital raise that signals the company needed emergency funding. The photonics/co-packaged optics technology is genuinely interesting for the AI data center buildout, and if POET can establish new customer relationships and demonstrate product traction, the stock could recover — but the current risk/reward skews negative until at least one of these three overhangs is resolved. **Maintain cautious posture. No new buying until order pipeline visibility improves or litigation is dismissed.**
