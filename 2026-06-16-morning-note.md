## Macro Backdrop

The dominant overnight catalyst is the US-Iran ceasefire: President Trump announced an agreement Monday to end the conflict, lift the US blockade on Iranian oil, and reopen the Strait of Hormuz by the end of the week. The de-escalation triggered a broad risk-on move — crude tumbled more than 5% to a two-month low, and equities rallied hard into the close, led by tech. The S&P 500 closed at a fresh high (7,554.29, +1.65%) and the Nasdaq jumped 3.07% to 26,683.94 in its best day since March, with SpaceX extending its post-IPO rally (+~20%) as the standout mover.

On the data front, May CPI printed at **4.25% YoY**, the hottest reading in nearly three years, driven by "supercore" services, gasoline, and electricity. Despite the inflation overshoot, the 10-year Treasury yield eased to **~4.43%**, a one-month low, as falling oil prices reduced near-term inflation risk premium. The Fed — now under incoming Chair Kevin Warsh, who faces the most divided FOMC in 30+ years — holds its policy meeting **Wednesday** and is widely expected to leave the funds rate unchanged at 3.50%–3.75%, though rate traders still lean toward one hike later this year given the CPI print. Gold is elevated near $4,309/oz and likely to stay volatile into the Fed decision.

## Trade Ideas

1. **Long airlines/transports (JETS, XLI) on cheaper crude** — WTI's slide to ~$80 on the ceasefire materially improves the fuel-cost outlook for transports; this is a direct, low-friction way to play the de-escalation.

2. **Fade the energy-equity complex (XLE) near-term** — With Brent/WTI down on reduced geopolitical risk premium and the Strait of Hormuz reopening, upstream E&P names lose their conflict-driven tailwind; watch for further downside if the ceasefire holds.

3. **Stay long mega-cap tech/AI momentum** — Nasdaq's 3%+ rally and SpaceX's debut strength suggest risk appetite is intact; pullbacks into Wednesday's FOMC are likely buyable unless Warsh surprises hawkish.

4. **Hedge into the FOMC** — With CPI at 4.25% YoY but yields falling on oil-driven disinflation hopes, the setup is two-sided. Short-dated options around Wednesday's decision are a reasonable hedge against a "higher for longer" surprise from a still-divided committee.

## POET Technologies (POET) Daily Update

**Current Price:** $13.94 (June 15 close; intraday range $12.84–$15.08)
**Daily Change:** Down sharply from recent highs — shares slid roughly 10% after the $400M offering closed, even as the stock remains up ~87% YTD and was the AI-photonics momentum name of May (+72.6%).

### News Last 24 Hours
POET closed its previously announced **US$400M non-brokered registered direct offering** with a single institutional investor at a modest premium to market. Management is framing the proceeds as funding a roughly **10x expansion** in manufacturing capacity (wafer fab + optical-engine assembly), accelerated hiring, and new facilities, with guidance to ship 30,000+ optical engines in 2026 and ~$430M in cash to fund the Malaysia ramp. No new SEC filings beyond the offering closing 6-K were identified in the last 24 hours.

### Key Risk Status

| Risk | Status |
|---|---|
| **Marvell/Celestial AI order cancellation & NDA lawsuit** | **Active/Unresolved.** Marvell (which acquired Celestial AI) cancelled all purchase orders April 23–27, citing POET's CFO disclosing confidential shipment/invoice details in an April 21 video interview. Stock fell 47.3% on the announcement; no settlement disclosed. |
| **Securities class actions (PFIC tax misrepresentation)** | **Active.** Multiple firms (Rosen, Bronstein Gewirtz, Faruqi, Block & Leviton) have filed/are pursuing suits over the April 1–27, 2026 class period alleging understated PFIC classification risk and NDA-related disclosure failures. Lead plaintiff deadline: **June 29, 2026.** |
| **$400M raise dilution impact** | **Material, now closed.** 19,047,620 new shares issued plus a matching 3-year warrant at $21.00 strike; full warrant exercise would add another 19,047,620 shares (~38.1M shares of total potential dilution). Stock dropped ~10% on the closing news as the warrant overhang weighed on sentiment. |

### Outlook: **Neutral, leaning cautious**
The capital raise gives POET real runway to scale manufacturing into AI-photonics demand, but the stock faces a cluster of binary catalysts — the June 26 annual meeting and June 29 lead-plaintiff deadline — with the Marvell/Celestial relationship still severed and litigation unresolved; we'd wait for legal clarity before adding exposure.
