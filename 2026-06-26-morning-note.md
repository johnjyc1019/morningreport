## Macro Backdrop

Risk sentiment is mixed heading into the final session of the week. US equity futures are under modest pressure as Apple's announced price hikes on iPads and MacBooks weigh on broad tech, partially offsetting the tailwind from Micron's blockbuster earnings. Inflation remains the dominant macro narrative: CPI ran at 3.8% YoY in April (highest since May 2023), driven by the energy shock following the February onset of the US–Iran conflict. The Fed left rates unchanged at 3.50–3.75% at its June meeting but updated dot-plot projections shifted hawkishly, with markets now pricing ~68% odds of a September hike (up from 29% a week ago). A significant geopolitical breakthrough — President Trump signing an agreement with Iran paving the way for reopening of the Strait of Hormuz — is easing oil prices and may reduce near-term inflationary pressure, offering the Fed some room. University of Michigan inflation expectations data is due today and could move Treasuries.

## POET Technologies (POET) Daily Update

**Price:** ~$11.13 (June 24 close) | Range this week: $10.35–$11.65
**Change from June 4 peak (~$15.48):** approx. -28% over ~3 weeks

### News (Last 24 Hours)
- **AGM today (June 26):** POET's Annual General and Special Meeting is scheduled for today. Key agenda item: shareholder vote on redomiciling the company from Canada to the United States. Outcome could affect future US investor access and index eligibility.
- No new press releases or SEC filings identified overnight as of this writing.

### Key Risk Status

| Risk | Status |
|---|---|
| **Marvell/Celestial AI order cancellation & NDA lawsuit** | ACTIVE — HIGH. On April 23, 2026, Marvell issued written cancellation of all POs from Celestial AI (its subsidiary), citing NDA breach by CEO Thomas Mika, who discussed shipping timelines in a public YouTube interview on April 21. Stock fell 47.3% on April 27 disclosure. No settlement or resolution reported. NDA breach claim remains outstanding and could expose POET to countersuit or further commercial damage. |
| **Securities class actions (PFIC tax misrepresentation)** | ACTIVE — HIGH, DEADLINE IMMINENT. Multiple law firms (Rosen, Kirby McInerney, Levi & Korsinsky, Faruqi & Faruqi, Bragar Eagel & Squire) have filed securities class actions alleging POET failed to disclose its likely Passive Foreign Investment Company (PFIC) status, which carries adverse US tax consequences for shareholders. Class period: April 1–27, 2026. **Lead plaintiff deadline: June 29, 2026 (3 days away).** Post-deadline, cases consolidate and litigation risk crystalizes. |
| **$400M raise dilution impact** | ONGOING — MODERATE. On May 18, 2026, POET closed a registered direct offering at $21.00/share, issuing 19,047,620 shares plus warrants (exercisable at $26.15 for 3 years) for gross proceeds of $400M. Immediate dilution ~10–12%; full warrant exercise would roughly double the new share count (38M+ new shares). Stock currently trades well below the $21 offering price, creating ongoing selling pressure from institutional buyers marked down. |

### Additional Context
- POET signed a supply agreement with Lumilens post-crisis with an initial $50M PO and a framework reaching $500M+ over 5 years — this is the key bull case offset to Marvell cancellation.
- Q1 2026 revenue: $503K (up from $167K YoY) — still pre-revenue at meaningful scale.

### Outlook: **BEARISH near-term**
Three concurrent headwinds — active litigation with a near-term catalyst (June 29 lead plaintiff deadline), ongoing NDA dispute with no resolution, and a stock trading 47%+ below pre-crisis levels with warrant overhang — create sustained selling pressure. The Lumilens deal and AGM redomicile are potential positive catalysts but are unlikely to overcome legal and dilution overhangs in the short term.
