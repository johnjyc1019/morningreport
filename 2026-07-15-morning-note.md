## Macro Backdrop

June CPI came in at **-0.4% MoM / +3.5% YoY** (consensus: +3.8%), the biggest monthly deflation print in recent memory and a meaningful dovish surprise. July Fed hike odds collapsed from ~42% to ~17% overnight. The 10-year Treasury yield sits at **4.583%** (-2 bps). Risk sentiment is broadly positive this morning despite two competing headwinds: (1) Trump's Strait of Hormuz blockade announcement Monday sent oil +4.8% and dragged equities lower; (2) IBM's catastrophic Q2 pre-announcement (-22–26%) rattled enterprise software. Both drags are being absorbed by the CPI tailwind, with futures pointing higher on the open. New Fed Chair Kevin Warsh has scrapped forward guidance, making each data print incrementally more important.
