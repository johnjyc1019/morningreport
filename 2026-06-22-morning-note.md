## Macro Backdrop

US equity futures are softer to start the week, with the S&P 500 down ~0.4%, Nasdaq 100 off ~0.6%, and Dow futures down ~0.3%. The primary headwind is renewed US-Iran tension as diplomatic talks kick off Monday, pushing crude oil higher and reviving risk-off sentiment. Last week's Federal Reserve meeting (first under new Chair Kevin Warsh) produced a hold at 3.50%–3.75% but struck a hawkish tone — the updated SEP showed roughly half of policymakers penciling in at least one additional hike in 2026, and markets are now fully pricing a rate increase by October. CPI was reported at 4.25% YoY in the May print (above the Fed's 2% target), driven by "supercore" services, energy, and electricity costs. The 10-year Treasury yield sits at approximately 4.46%. Offsetting some macro drag: an Apple-Intel foundry deal announced late last week sent semiconductors sharply higher, and the broader AI capex buildout continues to underpin technology sentiment.

## POET Technologies (POET) Daily Update

**Current Price:** ~$12.15 (last close June 20, 2026); traded $11.59–$12.43 on June 20. Peaked at $14.77 on June 15 (+17.9% that day). Down sharply from the ~$21 offering price of its May capital raise. *Real-time quote not available; check live price.*

**24-Hour News:** No major new filings or press releases identified in the last 24 hours as of this writing. The June 29 class-action lead-plaintiff deadline is the next hard catalyst.

### Key Risk Status

| Risk | Status |
|---|---|
| **(1) Marvell/Celestial AI order cancellation & NDA lawsuit** | ACTIVE — Marvell canceled all POs on April 23, 2026; cited NDA breach by CFO Thomas Mika, who disclosed Celestial AI as a customer in a public interview. Stock fell 47.3% on April 27 disclosure. POET disputes the characterization; no resolution announced. |
| **(2) Securities class actions (PFIC tax misrepresentation)** | ACTIVE — Multiple firms (Rosen, Faruqi & Faruqi, Bronstein Gewirtz, Bragar Eagel) filed class actions alleging POET misrepresented its Passive Foreign Investment Company (PFIC) status, which carries adverse U.S. tax consequences for shareholders, and that the NDA breach was undisclosed. Lead-plaintiff deadline: **June 29, 2026**. |
| **(3) $400M raise dilution impact** | ONGOING — Offering (closed May 18) issued ~19.05M shares at $21 + warrants for ~19.05M more shares at $26.15 (3-year). At current ~$12 price, both tranches are deeply out of the money, but the share count overhang (~38M potential new shares vs. prior float) remains a structural ceiling on near-term price appreciation. |

### Outlook: BEARISH (near-term)

The stock has recovered meaningfully from its April 27 crash lows on the back of the Lumilens partnership announcement and capital raise, but three unresolved headwinds — the Marvell NDA dispute, active multi-firm securities litigation, and heavy dilution from the May offering — make a sustained re-rating unlikely before the June 29 class-action deadline passes and legal clarity emerges. Risk/reward skews unfavorable into that date.
