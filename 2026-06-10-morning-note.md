## Macro Backdrop

The dominant theme today is the May CPI print due at **8:30am ET** — the first major inflation read under Fed Chair Kevin Warsh's watch, and potentially a market mover in either direction. April CPI came in hot at **+3.8% YoY / +0.6% MoM**, the highest reading since May 2023, leaving markets on edge. The Fed's target range sits at **3.50%–3.75%**; the next FOMC meeting is **June 16–17**, and any upside surprise today could meaningfully reprice rate-cut expectations.

Brent crude remains elevated around **$108/barrel** (WTI ~$104) as markets assess the durability of the US-Iran ceasefire. The Strait of Hormuz risk premium has not fully unwound. The **10-year Treasury yield** is anchored near **4.57%**, hovering close to a two-week high, while the 30-year briefly topped 5.1% in mid-May.

Tuesday saw a rotation out of heavyweight tech. Nvidia, AMD, and Oracle fell 1–3%. Apple shed 3% after the EU blocked its Siri AI assistant rollout on antitrust grounds. The S&P 500 slipped ~1%, Nasdaq 100 ~2%, and the Dow ~0.5%, pulling the S&P to its lowest level since May 2026.

## Trade Ideas

1. **Fade tech rallies on CPI surprise risk.** With the Nasdaq having already sold off and May CPI printing today, a hotter-than-expected number (~3.9%+ YoY) could trigger another leg lower in rate-sensitive growth names. Consider puts or underweights on QQQ ahead of 8:30am.

2. **Energy remains a macro hedge.** Brent above $100 with Iranian ceasefire fragility supports XLE and integrated majors. The ceasefire is fragile — 82% of Polymarket participants are skeptical of a sustained agreement.

3. **Defensives as CPI hedge.** SJM's beat illustrates pricing power in staples. KO, PG, and other consumer staples offer shelter if the macro deteriorates further on hot inflation.

4. **Watch Treasury short end.** A hot CPI print compresses near-term cut expectations sharply. SRVR or TBTF put spreads on 2-year notes may offer asymmetric reward.

## POET Technologies (POET) Daily Update

**Current Price:** ~$12.53 premarket (+5.20% vs. Monday close of $12.41)
**Prior Session Range:** $11.77 – $13.07
**Market Cap:** ~$2.12B | **52-week Range:** $3.87 – $20.81

### News (Last 24 Hours)
No material new press releases or SEC filings since Monday's close. Premarket bid appears to reflect continued consolidation and retail accumulation following the June/May volatility. Collaboration with **Lumilens Inc.** on wafer-level photonic integration for AI optical networks remains the most recent positive catalyst (announced prior to June 10).

### Key Risk Status

| Risk | Status |
|---|---|
| **1. Marvell/Celestial AI order cancellation & NDA lawsuit** | Marvell (post-Celestial AI acquisition) cancelled all POET purchase orders on April 23, 2026 (disclosed April 27). Marvell cited POET's violation of NDA confidentiality obligations — a POET executive disclosed invoice/shipping details in a public video interview. The stock crashed **47.3%** on April 27. No settlement or reinstatement announced. Active litigation risk. |
| **2. Securities class actions (PFIC tax misrepresentation)** | Multiple firms (Rosen Law, Bronstein Gewirtz, SueWallSt) filed class actions covering April 1–27, 2026. Allegations: POET failed to disclose its PFIC (Passive Foreign Investment Company) status, creating adverse U.S. tax consequences for shareholders and overstating valuation. Lead plaintiff deadline: **June 29, 2026**. POET is proactively redomiciling to the U.S. and offering QEF election info to mitigate PFIC consequences. |
| **3. $400M capital raise dilution** | POET closed a $400M registered direct offering on May 18, 2026 at $21.00/share (19,047,620 shares + matching warrants at $26.25 strike, 3-year term). Stock fell ~22% on announcement. With ~$500K quarterly revenue, the raise represents extreme dilution relative to current operations — albeit it funds manufacturing scale-up, R&D, and potential acquisitions. |

### Outlook: **Neutral (with negative bias near-term)**
POET is burning cash against minimal revenue, carries multiple active class-action lawsuits with the June 29 lead plaintiff deadline as an overhang, and lost its most prominent commercial relationship (Celestial AI/Marvell) due to an NDA breach. The $400M raise buys time and optionality, but the path to revenue re-acceleration is unclear without a new anchor customer. Any new design-win announcement or settlement clarity would be bullish catalysts; continued lawsuit escalation or absence of commercial news is bearish.
