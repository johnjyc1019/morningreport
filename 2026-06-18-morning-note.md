## Macro Backdrop

Markets enter Wednesday in a cautious but constructive tone as all eyes turn to the first Federal Reserve policy meeting under new Chairman **Kevin Warsh**. Rates are universally expected to stay unchanged, but Warsh's tone and the updated dot plot will dominate the afternoon. With May CPI printing a hotter-than-expected **4.25%** — driven by supercore services, AI-related electricity demand, and gasoline — nearly half of FOMC members now project at least one rate hike this year. Warsh has signaled skepticism toward forward guidance, so any hawkish inflection in language could reprice the long end. The **10-year Treasury yield** sits at ~**4.46%**, uncomfortably close to the May CPI print and flirting with a negative real yield.

On the macro data front, **May retail sales surged +0.9%** (vs. +0.5% expected), reinforcing the "no landing" narrative even as equities remain volatile. A potential **US-Iran peace agreement** — expected to be signed June 20 — is weighing on crude oil, with WTI sliding ~1.7% and Brent holding sub-$79. EU-China trade tensions remain simmering as von der Leyen outlines new trade defense tools at this week's EU summit, with Beijing warning of retaliation.

## POET Technologies (POET) Daily Update

**Current Price:** ~$12.83 (June 17 close) | **YTD:** deeply negative from April highs (~$22+)

### News in Last 24 Hours
No material new filings or press releases confirmed for June 18. The stock has been whipsawing between $12–$15 since mid-June. The prior week's Lumilens order announcement ($50M initial PO, up to $500M over five years) remains the key bullish catalyst. The June 29 class action lead-plaintiff deadline is the next significant date.

### Key Risk Status

| Risk | Status |
|---|---|
| **Marvell/Celestial AI order cancellation & NDA lawsuit** | **Active / Unresolved.** Marvell cancelled all purchase orders April 27 after POET's CFO disclosed confidential order details in a Stocktwits YouTube interview, breaching NDA. Stock fell 47.3% on the announcement. No settlement disclosed; NDA breach claim stands. |
| **Securities class actions (PFIC tax misrepresentation)** | **Active / Escalating.** Multiple firms (Rosen, Bronstein, Faruqi & Faruqi, RGRD Law) have filed actions covering the April 1–27, 2026 class period. Core allegation: POET knew PFIC classification was probable but used equivocal language in filings, misleading US investors on tax treatment. **Lead plaintiff deadline: June 29, 2026.** |
| **$400M raise dilution impact** | **Material / Ongoing.** 19.05M shares issued at $21.00 (May 18 close); stock now ~$12.83 — offering price is ~39% above current market. Warrants for another 19.05M shares exercisable at $26.15 for three years add further overhang (~38M total shares potential, representing significant dilution against the existing float). |

### Outlook: **NEUTRAL-to-BEARISH short-term / Speculative LONG for risk-tolerant accounts**

The Lumilens deal is a genuine commercial milestone that keeps the AI photonics thesis alive, but POET faces a triple overhang — unresolved NDA litigation, an imminent class action deadline, and a stock price 39% below its recent capital raise — making near-term upside catalysts unlikely to outweigh legal and dilution headwinds until at least July.
