## Macro Backdrop

US equities extended their post-holiday recovery on Monday, with the Dow surpassing 53,000 for a new all-time closing record. The S&P 500 (+0.72%) and Nasdaq 100 (+1.26%) were led by big-tech and chip stocks amid relatively light volume. Sentiment is broadly constructive heading into Wednesday's FOMC minutes and next week's CPI print.

The dominant macro themes remain: (1) a slowly normalizing Strait of Hormuz dragging crude lower, (2) a Fed on hold but increasingly leaning toward a September cut, and (3) AI-driven capex continuing to support mega-cap tech multiples despite a mid-year chip-sector wobble.

- **10-yr Treasury yield:** ~4.47%, easing modestly from last week's highs ahead of FOMC minutes
- **CPI (latest print):** 3.8% YoY (April), highest since May 2023; next read due mid-July
- **Fed policy:** Chair Kevin Warsh signals no urgency to move; benchmark rate held at 3.50–3.75% since December 2025. Markets now pricing ~56% probability of a September cut (down from ~64% pre-jobs report)
- **Geopolitics:** Uneasy US-Israeli/Iran ceasefire in place since April 7 has eased energy tensions; Hormuz shipping traffic recovering (still ~5% of pre-conflict norms). OPEC+ signaling supply increases caps the crude upside

Without a credible new customer announcement or litigation resolution, the fundamental and technical picture remains deteriorating. The stock is in a structurally impaired zone.
