## Macro Backdrop

US equity futures are marginally lower in overnight trade as markets digest a complex backdrop of hot inflation, elevated Treasury yields, and a tentative thaw in US-Iran tensions. The S&P 500 is off a modest 0.08% while the Dow lags at -0.15%. Asian markets were the overnight standout — the Nikkei surged +3.54% on JPY weakness and positive tech sentiment following Nvidia's blowout print. European indices are broadly higher, tracking the tech-driven global risk-on tone.

The macro story remains dominated by sticky inflation and the rate outlook. April CPI came in at 3.8% YoY (core: 2.8%), well above the Fed's 2% target, with energy the primary culprit (+17.9% YoY) driven by the US-Iran conflict that has choked traffic through the Strait of Hormuz since March. The 10-year Treasury yield has spiked to approximately 4.6%-4.7%, near a multi-year high, after a heavy week of Treasury supply ($691B issued). The Fed has held rates at 3.5%-3.75% and markets now price virtually zero probability of cuts through end-2027 — with a 25% chance of an outright hike by year-end.

Oil is pulling back sharply today. Brent fell ~5.2% to ~$105.50 yesterday and WTI is trading ~$99-$100, as traders grow cautiously optimistic that Trump's postponement of a planned US military strike on Iran (delayed from today to allow negotiations) could lead to a diplomatic resolution and the reopening of Hormuz supply routes.

## Trade Ideas

1. **Long Crude Oil Puts / Short Energy near-term:** WTI has broken below $100. If Iran negotiations gain traction, the Hormuz risk premium (estimated +$20-30/bbl) partially unwinds. Buy Aug WTI puts at $95 strike for a geopolitical resolution hedge.

2. **Long NVDA on dips:** The Q1 beat and Q2 guide show AI chip demand remains robust. Any intraday weakness driven by profit-taking is a buying opportunity for investors with a 6-12 month horizon. Watch $120 as near-term support.

3. **Short 10-yr Treasuries (long TBT):** With CPI at 3.8%, the Fed on hold, and a massive Treasury issuance calendar, the path of least resistance for 10-year yields remains higher. Consider TBT or SPTS for a rates-higher expression.

4. **Watch WMT today:** Walmart reports pre-market. In a 3.8% inflation environment with consumer stress, any commentary on trade-down behavior, tariff pass-through, or margin compression will set the tone for the entire consumer sector. Beat + positive comp guidance = broad retail lift; a miss would drag ROST, TGT, and DG.

5. **Avoid POET (POET):** Multiple unresolved legal and structural risks (see section below) make this speculative. Not a trading vehicle for most institutional mandates.

## POET Technologies (POET) Daily Update

**Current Price:** ~$13.21 | **Daily Change:** ~+1.1% (after -8.0% on May 19 from $14.21 → $13.07)
**52-Week Range:** $3.87 – $20.81 | **Market Cap:** ~$2B

### Recent Developments (Last 24 Hours)
No material new filings or press releases overnight. The stock appears to be stabilizing after a brutal three-week sequence: April 27 order-cancellation collapse (-47.3%), May 18 capital raise/close (+22% premium priced, then stock -22% on closing day), and May 19 follow-through selling (-8%). The stock has given back roughly 35% from its pre-raise close levels.

### Key Risk Status

| Risk                                       | Status                                                                                                    |
|--------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Marvell/Celestial AI order cancellation & NDA lawsuit** | **ACTIVE / MATERIAL.** Marvell (via Celestial AI acquisition) cancelled all purchase orders on April 27 citing POET's CFO publicly disclosing confidential supply relationship details on an investor call — a direct NDA violation. The cancellation triggered the 47.3% single-day share collapse. Litigation risk from Marvell/Celestial AI remains unresolved. This is existential for POET's near-term revenue pipeline. |
| **Securities class actions (PFIC tax misrepresentation)** | **ACTIVE.** Multiple law firms (Rosen Law Firm, Faruqi & Faruqi, Block & Leviton) have filed or are investigating securities class actions. The class period is April 1–April 27, 2026. Core allegation: POET failed to disclose material risk of PFIC classification, exposing US investors to adverse tax treatment. Lead plaintiff deadline: **June 29, 2026**. POET has announced a QEF election option and plans to redomicile to the US to eliminate future PFIC risk — but litigation proceeds regardless. |
| **$400M raise dilution impact**            | **SIGNIFICANT.** The May 18 raise issued 19,047,620 new shares + 19,047,620 warrants (exercisable over 3 years) to MMCAP International at $21.00/unit. The combined dilution — if warrants are exercised — could be ~38M shares, representing substantial dilution to existing shareholders. Stock fell 22% on the closing day and another 8% the next session as the market priced in this dilution risk. Proceeds earmarked for manufacturing expansion, M&A, R&D, and working capital. |

### Outlook: **NEUTRAL-TO-BEARISH** (Short-Term)

The $400M war chest gives POET capital to execute its photonic AI manufacturing roadmap and potentially acquire new customers, but the near-term technical picture is damaged. Legal overhangs (NDA breach, securities class actions), a depleted near-term revenue pipeline (Celestial AI cancellations), and severe dilution create headwinds that are likely to limit meaningful upside until: (1) a new anchor customer is announced, and (2) at least one securities class action is dismissed or settled. For now, POET is an event-driven speculative play — not a core holding.
