# Morning Note — June 29, 2026

---

## Macro Backdrop

Risk sentiment is broadly cautious into the week's final session. The S&P 500 and Nasdaq 100 are tracking weekly losses, weighed by renewed investor skepticism around AI capital expenditure payback timelines for the Magnificent Seven. The Dow and Russell 2000 are modestly positive on the week, suggesting a rotation toward value and small-caps.

**Inflation** remains the dominant macro overhang. The May CPI print came in at +0.5% MoM / +4.2% YoY — the hottest reading since 2023. Core PCE rose to 3.4%, well above the Fed's 2% target. Markets are now pricing ~3 Fed hikes this year, with ~62% probability of the first move in September. New Fed Chair Kevin Warsh has framed policy credibility as paramount, leaving little room for dovish pivots.

**Rates:** 10-year Treasury yields have retreated to ~4.41%, down >8bps since last week's peak, as energy prices collapse.

**Energy:** Brent crude fell 4% to ~$73/bbl and WTI to ~$70/bbl — near pre-Iran-war lows — as fears of supply disruption ease. Oil weakness is a modest deflationary tailwind but highlights the severity of the ongoing Middle East conflict and its uncertain trajectory.

**Geopolitics:** The US/Israel-Iran war (initiated late February) remains the dominant macro geopolitical event. Energy transmission through the Strait of Hormuz is disrupted; shipping/insurance premia are elevated. Europe and energy-importing EMs are most exposed. The US is relatively insulated but faces confidence and financial channel spillovers.

## Trade Ideas

1. **S&P 500 vs. Russell 2000 (Long RUT / Short SPY):** The rotation from Mag 7 into small-caps appears nascent but meaningful. If the Fed hike path reprices higher, quality small-caps with domestic revenue exposure could continue outperforming. Risk: credit conditions tighten with rate hikes, squeezing small-cap balance sheets.

2. **Long Energy (XLE) on dip:** WTI at ~$70 reflects a lot of geopolitical de-escalation that hasn't happened. Any escalation in the Strait of Hormuz reverses this quickly. Reward/risk is attractive for a tactical bounce.

3. **Short Nasdaq 100 (QQQ) / Long DJIA (DIA):** Mag 7 AI capex fears are not yet fully priced; further multiple compression is possible as FCF yield looks thin at current valuations. The Dow offers a defensive value buffer.
