## 6. POET Technologies (POET) Daily Update

**Current Price:** ~$14.59 (as of May 22 close) | **From May 14 Peak:** -28% | **1-Month:** +72%

### Recent News (Last 24–48 hrs)
No new material filings or press releases detected overnight. Stock has been in a consolidation/decline phase since the $400M raise closed May 18.

### Key Risk Status

| Risk | Status |
|---|---|
| **Marvell/Celestial AI Order Cancellation & NDA Lawsuit** | ⚠️ **ACTIVE / UNRESOLVED.** Marvell (via Celestial AI acquisition) cancelled ALL purchase orders on April 23, 2026, citing POET CFO's alleged violation of confidentiality obligations during a public interview disclosing shipment details. Stock plunged 47.3% on April 27. No settlement or reinstatement reported. This was POET's primary revenue-generating relationship. |
| **Securities Class Actions (PFIC Tax Misrepresentation)** | ⚠️ **MULTIPLE SUITS ACTIVE.** Rosen Law, Levi & Korsinsky, Block & Leviton and others allege POET misrepresented its PFIC (Passive Foreign Investment Company) status and failed to disclose adverse U.S. tax consequences. POET confirmed it will likely be classified as a PFIC for FY2025. Lead plaintiff deadline: **June 29, 2026**. |
| **$400M Capital Raise Dilution** | ⚠️ **CLOSED / DILUTION IN MARKET.** $400M direct offering to MMCAP International closed May 18 at $21.00/share (19,047,620 shares + equal warrant). Stock fell 22% on close day. Full warrant exercise would roughly double the dilution. Proceeds earmarked for manufacturing buildout, M&A, and R&D. |

### Q1 2026 Financials (Most Recent Quarter)
- Revenue: $503,389 — beat estimates (but near-zero scale)
- EPS: -$0.08 — missed estimates
- Net Loss: $12.34 million
- New COO: Sandeep Kumar appointed May 11
- Lumilens JDA: $50M initial purchase order (new customer offset to Marvell loss)

### Outlook: 🔴 BEARISH

POET faces a perfect storm of legal, financial, and operational headwinds. The loss of the Marvell/Celestial AI relationship removed the only meaningful near-term revenue catalyst, while three simultaneous securities class actions and a confirmed PFIC designation create material legal liability and ongoing shareholder churn. The $400M raise provides a multi-year cash runway but the dilution (38M+ potential new shares versus a pre-deal float) severely limits near-term upside. Revenue remains sub-$1M/quarter. Until POET demonstrates it can replace the Marvell/Celestial AI volume with concrete, deliverable orders — and until the class action litigation resolves — the risk/reward is unfavorable.
