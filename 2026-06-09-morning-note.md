## Macro Backdrop

The macro regime remains firmly risk-off with an inflationary tilt. April CPI printed at **3.8% YoY** (highest since May 2023), and the May CPI report drops tomorrow (Wednesday, June 10) at 8:30 AM ET — the single most important data release of the week. The **10-year Treasury yield** settled around **4.54%** after a stronger-than-expected May jobs report repriced rate-cut expectations; markets now price just one cut (or none) by December 2026, versus the two the Fed telegraphed earlier this year. Under new Fed Chair **Kevin Warsh**, the FOMC is widely expected to hold the **fed funds rate at 3.50–3.75%** at the June 16–17 meeting. **Brent crude remains above $100/bbl**, propped up by Middle East tensions that have reduced Strait of Hormuz traffic to ~5% of pre-conflict levels following the February 28 US-Israel-Iran confrontation. Energy is the year's top-performing sector (+~40% YTD). The Supreme Court is expected to rule imminently on challenges to the administration's "Reciprocal Tariff" executive orders — a binary catalyst for trade-sensitive names.

## POET Technologies (POET) Daily Update

**Current Price:** ~$12.41 | **Daily Change:** ~-3.5% in June 2026 | **YTD:** +87%

### Last 24-Hour News & Filings
- No new material 6-K filings detected in the last 24 hours. Most recent filing was the May 15 6-K covering Q1 2026 results: **net loss of $12.3M**, cash and short-term investments **$429.1M** (funded by the $400M raise). R&D spend remains elevated on 800G/1.6T optical engines and AI light-source products.
- The Motley Fool published a June 8 analysis asking why POET "skyrocketed 72.6% last month but is sinking in June" — consistent with post-raise/post-Lumilens-order momentum fading into legal overhang.
- COO Dr. Sandeep Kumar (appointed May 11) is still in the news-flow integration phase; no material operational announcements yet.

### Key Risk Status

| Risk                                      | Status                                                                                                                                                      |
|-------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Marvell / Celestial AI order cancellation & NDA lawsuit** | **Active & unresolved.** On April 27, Marvell canceled all Celestial AI purchase orders, citing POET's alleged breach of confidentiality after CEO Mika discussed shipment details in a public YouTube interview on April 21. Stock fell 47.3% on the disclosure. No settlement or legal resolution announced. |
| **Securities class actions (PFIC / NDA misrepresentation)** | **Active.** Multiple firms (Rosen, Robbins, Levi & Korsinsky, Bronstein Gewirtz, SueWallSt) filed; class period April 1–27, 2026. Core allegation: POET failed to adequately disclose its likely PFIC status (negative US tax implications for shareholders) and concealed the NDA breach risk. **Lead-plaintiff deadline: June 29, 2026.** |
| **$400M capital raise dilution impact**   | **Closed; dilution absorbed.** The raise closed and is reflected in $429.1M of cash/short-term investments on the Q1 balance sheet. The stock's +87% YTD performance suggests the market is partially pricing in optionality on the Lumilens deal ($50M initial order, potentially $500M total). Dilution is a known quantity; execution risk is now the primary concern. |

### Outlook: **NEUTRAL / CAUTIOUS**
POET has a credible long-term story in AI optical interconnects and a fortified balance sheet, but the stock is uninvestable for most institutional buyers until the class action legal process clarifies (deadline June 29). The Lumilens partnership is a genuine catalyst if orders ramp, but the loss of the Marvell/Celestial AI relationship and the accompanying governance questions (public NDA disclosure by the CEO) create a sentiment ceiling. Watch for any pre-June 29 legal developments or new customer announcements.
