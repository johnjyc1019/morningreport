## Macro Backdrop

The macro environment entering Wednesday remains cautious. The Federal Reserve held the fed funds rate at **3.50–3.75%** for a fourth consecutive meeting at its June FOMC, but sticky inflation — running at approximately **4.25%** versus the 2% target — is keeping rate-hike expectations alive. Market-implied odds of a September hike have crept up to ~58% following a renewed rise in oil prices. The 10-year Treasury yield moved to **~4.50%**, a two-week high, reflecting renewed inflation anxiety. The dominant macro overhang remains the unresolved Middle East conflict: a US-Israeli ceasefire with Iran took hold April 7, but Strait of Hormuz shipping remains at just **5% of pre-conflict levels**, a structural drag on global energy flows. The IMF's July 2026 World Economic Outlook flags "amplification risks" from the Middle East war, and Fed leadership transition uncertainty adds further noise heading into midterms.

## Trade Ideas

1. **Long Energy (XLE / Brent-linked producers):** Strait of Hormuz at 5% of normal flow is a structurally bullish crude catalyst. Oil price renewed strength supports integrated majors and E&Ps with Middle East exposure hedged. Risk: ceasefire broadening could unwind supply premium quickly.

2. **Short Rate-Sensitive Growth (IWM / XLY):** With the 10yr back at 4.50% and September hike odds at 58%, small-caps and consumer discretionary face multiple compression. Duration risk is repricing. Risk: soft CPI print could quickly reverse.

3. **Long Defense / Aerospace (ITA):** Elevated geopolitical tensions, ongoing Middle East conflict, and increased European defense spending (post-China overcapacity tensions) support sustained order flow. Secular theme with bipartisan political backing.

4. **Monitor Micron (MU) Pullbacks:** Record quarter validates AI memory demand cycle. Any broader chip-sector sell-off (note: chips fell early last week) may offer re-entry.

### Outlook: **BEARISH**
Multiple compounding headwinds — loss of primary revenue-generating customer, active securities class actions in discovery, shares trading at a 57% discount to the last capital raise price, and significant warrant overhang — create a difficult near-term setup. The production ramp story is intact on paper, but without a named replacement customer for Celestial AI volumes, the commercial validation that justified the valuation is absent. Speculative only; not suitable for risk-averse portfolios.
