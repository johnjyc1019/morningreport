## POET Technologies (POET) Daily Update

**Current Price:** Whipsawing — most recent comparable data: stock crashed ~47–49% on April 27–28 (from ~$8 to ~$4–5 range) on Marvell/Celestial AI cancellation news. POET subsequently soared on May 26 on the Lumilens deal + $400M raise (offering priced at $21/unit), indicating a dramatic recovery through May. As of June 1, the stock reportedly "soared" again on Lumilens/raise momentum. *Check live quote before trading — this name remains highly volatile.*

**Last 24 Hours:** No new SEC filings identified for June 2 specifically. Most recent significant filing was Form 6-K related to the $400M direct offering (closed May 18, 2026).

**Key Risk Status:**

1. **Marvell/Celestial AI Order Cancellation & NDA Lawsuit** — *Active / Ongoing.*
Marvell (which acquired Celestial AI) canceled all POET purchase orders on April 23, 2026, citing that POET had publicly disclosed confidential information about the PO in violation of its NDA. POET disclosed this on April 27, causing the ~47% crash. No settlement or resolution reported to date.

2. **Securities Class Actions (PFIC Tax Misrepresentation)** — *Active / Lead Plaintiff Deadline June 29.*
Multiple law firms (Rosen, Block & Leviton, RGRD) have filed or launched probes. Core allegation: POET misrepresented its PFIC (Passive Foreign Investment Company) tax status and that a senior executive breached a confidentiality agreement publicly. Class period: April 1–April 27, 2026. POET counters it will offer QEF election data and plans to redomicile to the US to eliminate future PFIC classification. Material litigation risk remains.

3. **$400M Raise Dilution Impact** — *Closed / Dilution Locked In.*
The May 18 direct offering issued 19,047,620 new shares at $21/unit plus a 3-year warrant exercisable for another 19,047,620 shares at $26.25/share. Combined, this represents significant potential dilution (~38M shares total if warrants exercised). Proceeds earmarked for manufacturing expansion, R&D scale-up, and potential acquisitions. Near-term dilution pressure is real but the capital runway is now substantially extended.

**Outlook: NEUTRAL-to-CAUTIOUSLY BULLISH — but only for high-risk-tolerant accounts.**
The Lumilens deal ($50M initial with path to $500M) and $400M war chest are genuine positives that give POET credibility and runway into the 2027 high-volume production ramp. However, three active legal overhangs (NDA lawsuit, multiple securities class actions with a June 29 deadline) will suppress institutional buying and create headline risk. Until at least one legal matter resolves favorably, this is a trader's stock, not an investor's stock. Size accordingly.
