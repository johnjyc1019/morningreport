Risk assets are under pressure to start the week as a toxic trifecta — surging energy costs, sticky inflation, and escalating Middle East tensions — weighs on sentiment. Friday's losses on Wall Street are extending into Monday's Asian session.

## Trade Ideas

1. **Long Energy (XLE / BP / Shell):** Brent above $100 with Strait of Hormuz at standstill and Iran ceasefire talks stalled. FTSE energy names are an Atlantic alternative to US E&P. Risk: sudden diplomatic breakthrough.

2. **Short Tech duration plays / Long value:** Rising real yields (10Y approaching 4.6%) continue to pressure long-duration growth stocks. Rotate toward value, energy, and industrials with pricing power. Meta's premarket -9% is a data point on CapEx multiple compression.

3. **Short Treasuries / Long TIPS:** With CPI at 3.8% and the Fed stuck, real yields remain a headwind. TIPS offer inflation protection if energy-driven CPI persists. Watch 10Y approaching 4.48% resistance.

4. **Defensive tilt — Healthcare:** UNH and Quest both beat-and-raised. Healthcare offers earnings visibility and relative insulation from energy/geopolitical shocks. Sector outperforms in stagflationary regimes.

5. **Gold:** Kinross premarket surge signals the market is reconnecting gold with geopolitical risk premium. GLD/GDX worth monitoring on Iran escalation headlines.
