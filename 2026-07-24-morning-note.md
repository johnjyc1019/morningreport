## Macro Backdrop

US equities suffered their worst single-session decline in a month on Thursday, July 23, as a toxic combination of Big Tech AI capex shock, surging crude oil, and escalating Middle East tensions hammered risk appetite. The S&P 500 fell 1.21% to 7,408.30, the Nasdaq Composite dropped 2.15% to 25,137.69, and the Dow shed 507 points (-0.97%) to 51,711.65. Brent crude surged above $100/bbl for the first time in two months after Houthi militants claimed attacks on Saudi oil tankers in the Red Sea, reigniting inflation concerns and lifting the 10-year Treasury yield to 4.71% — its highest level since January 2025, rising for a fourth consecutive session. The June CPI print offered a brief silver lining: headline CPI decelerated sharply to 3.5% YoY (from 4.2% in May), and core came in flat at 0% MoM vs. +0.2% expected. However, oil-driven inflation re-acceleration fears are now dominating, with markets pricing a >78% probability of a Fed rate hike in September.

### Outlook: BEARISH / HIGH-RISK

Despite management's constructive production ramp narrative and $830M in capital raised over 12 months, the near-term setup remains adverse: the Marvell/Celestial AI customer loss eliminates the primary near-term revenue driver, multiple securities class actions are in active litigation, the stock trades at 64% below its May offering price, and the broader market is in risk-off mode with rising rates. A re-rating higher would require either a credible new anchor customer announcement or a material positive resolution in the legal overhang — neither appears imminent. Proceed with caution; position sizing should reflect speculative-grade risk.
