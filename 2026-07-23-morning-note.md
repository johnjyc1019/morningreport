## Macro Backdrop

US equities face a soft open after a mixed big-tech earnings night. Alphabet beat on revenue but raised capex sharply and posted its first quarter of negative free cash flow; Tesla missed on margins. Futures are modestly lower. The geopolitical backdrop remains hot: US forces carried out an 11th consecutive night of airstrikes on Iran, keeping oil elevated and long-end yields near multi-month highs.

The June CPI print (released July 14) came in at **+3.5% YoY** vs. the 3.8% consensus, with core flat month-over-month — the coolest reading in over a year. The Fed has held at **3.50–3.75%** since June; markets now price only a ~15% chance of a July hike. However, crude's renewed strength (WTI spiked to ~$90/bbl in early June before retreating to ~$69) has revived inflation fears, pushing the 10-year Treasury back up to **~4.58–4.64%**.

JPMorgan CEO Jamie Dimon reiterated this week that investors are underpricing geopolitical and fiscal tail risks, and said he would not be a buyer of equities or long-dated Treasuries at current levels.

## Trade Ideas

**1. Long WAB (Westinghouse Air Brake) on momentum.** Rail infrastructure demand is secular; raised full-year guidance with strong margin expansion argues for multiple re-rating. Tactically attractive after a 10%+ gap — buy the first consolidation day.

**2. Fade GOOGL near term / buy the dip in 4–6 weeks.** The capex overhang is real ($44B in Q2 alone, first-ever negative FCF), and the market hasn't fully repriced the shift to heavy AI infrastructure spend. Cloud growth at +82% is impressive but consensus will revise numbers. Wait for the dust to settle before re-entering.

**3. Long defense ETFs (ITA / XAR).** The combination of ongoing US-Iran military action (night 11 of airstrikes) and UK Prime Minister Burnham installing a pro-spending Finance Minister are incremental tailwinds for Western defense budgets. Sentiment, not just fundamentals, drives these names in a geopolitical spike.

**4. Short crude / play oil mean reversion via puts on USO.** WTI has already pulled back from ~$90 to ~$69 on temporary tension relief; a fresh Iran escalation spike looks like a fade opportunity given the broader demand softness and US shale responsiveness.

## POET Technologies (POET) Daily Update

**Price (as of Jul 23, 2026):** ~$7.66 | **Daily change:** +3.93%

*Note: Price reflects most recent available quote; live premarket data may vary.*

### News (last 24h)
No new SEC filings or press releases detected in the last 24 hours. The stock continues to recover off its post-cancellation lows, supported by ongoing investor attention to POET's photonics partnership announcement and capacity expansion roadmap targeting **1 million optical engine units/month by end-2027**. The company has raised approximately **$830M in equity over the past year**, including a **$400M direct offering announced July 8, 2026** — the same day CEO/CFO Thomas Mika announced his retirement.

### Key Risk Status

| Risk | Status |
|---|---|
| **Marvell / Celestial AI order cancellation & NDA breach** | Marvell cancelled all Celestial AI purchase orders on April 23, 2026, citing CFO Mika's public Stocktwits disclosure of order/shipping information as a breach of confidentiality obligations. POET stock fell **~46%** on the disclosure (Apr 27). No public indication of NDA lawsuit resolution; the CFO who created the breach has since retired. Risk remains open — any litigation update is a material catalyst. |
| **Securities class action (PFIC tax misrepresentation)** | Multiple class action complaints filed late June 2026 alleging POET misrepresented its Passive Foreign Investment Company (PFIC) status to US investors. Cases appear to be in early stages. If meritorious, creates liability and reputational overhang. Active — monitor for lead plaintiff appointments and motion practice. |
| **$400M raise dilution impact** | The July 8 direct offering represents significant dilution at a moment of management transition (CEO retirement same day). The stock absorbed the news reasonably well but the higher share count suppresses near-term EPS and NAV-per-share metrics. Ongoing overhang until the capital is deployed productively into the capacity ramp. |

### Outlook: **Cautiously Neutral** (leaning bearish near term)
The recovery from April lows is real, driven by genuine photonics demand optionality, but three simultaneous overhangs — the Marvell NDA dispute, active securities class actions, and heavy post-raise dilution with a CEO vacancy — make it hard to build conviction ahead of newsflow. Wait for clarity on the class actions and the incoming management team before adding exposure.
