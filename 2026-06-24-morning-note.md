## Macro Backdrop

US equity futures are under renewed pressure this morning as a confluence of hawkish Fed signals, elevated yields, and semiconductor weakness weigh on risk sentiment. The Federal Reserve held the fed funds rate at **3.50%–3.75%** for a fourth consecutive meeting, but Chair Kevin Warsh delivered a distinctly hawkish press conference: 9 of 18 officials now see at least one rate hike in 2026, PCE inflation was revised sharply higher to **3.6%** for this year (from 2.7%), and the May CPI print confirmed headline inflation running at **+4.2% YoY**. The 10-year Treasury yield sits at **~4.50%**, its highest in roughly two weeks, with the 2-year above 4.20%—the highest since February 2025. Oil prices received some relief after President Trump signed an agreement with Iran paving the way for reopening the Strait of Hormuz, boosting expectations of faster supply recovery. The ZEW Economic Sentiment indicator turned positive for the first time since the start of the Middle East conflict, a nascent signal of improving European confidence, though energy pass-through remains a meaningful drag on global input costs.

## Trade Ideas

1. **Short semis into Micron print (MU puts / SOXS):** Macro headwinds (4.5% 10-yr, hawkish Fed, Korean Kospi chip rout) + crowded positioning + record consensus estimates = asymmetric downside risk if guidance disappoints or management commentary on AI order pace is cautious.

2. **Energy fade on Iran deal (short WTI via USO puts):** The US-Iran Strait of Hormuz agreement signals a faster supply recovery timeline. With oil prices still elevated relative to pre-conflict levels, the supply catalyst is underpriced. Short crude into the 60-day license window.

3. **Defensive rotation (XLP / XLU):** Hawkish Warsh Fed + elevated 10-yr + tech derating = textbook rotation into defensives. Consumer staples and utilities remain the cleaner risk-off expression ahead of the upcoming PCE print.
