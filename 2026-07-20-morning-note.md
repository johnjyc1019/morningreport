## Macro Backdrop

Risk-off sentiment carried into the weekend after Friday's sharp sell-off: S&P 500 -1.0%, Nasdaq -1.5%, Dow -407 pts as semiconductors cratered on fears that AI hyperscalers may trim capex following advances in Chinese AI (Moonshot's Kimi release). The AI sell-off follows a broader pattern of guidance disappointment in earnings season — beats are common, but forward outlooks are punishing stocks.

On the macro side, June CPI surprised to the downside: headline -0.4% MoM, 3.5% YoY (vs. 3.8% expected); core CPI 0% MoM, 2.6% YoY. The soft print drove the 10-year Treasury yield down to ~4.56% and slashed July Fed hike odds from 42% to 17%. September remains a live meeting (~60% probability of a hike).

Offsetting the dovish inflation read: President Trump reimposed a naval blockade on Iran (July 14), pushing Brent above $87/bbl and reviving stagflation fears. A deadly Middle East escalation over the weekend (July 18) adds further geopolitical risk heading into Monday's open.

## POET Technologies (POET) Daily Update

**Current Price:** ~$7.60–$8.16 (as of July 16–17, 2026) | **Daily Change:** Down ~10% during the week; down ~45–50% from mid-June high of ~$15

### Last 24 Hours
No confirmed new SEC filings or press releases as of this writing. Citadel entities' 5.1–5.9% passive stake disclosure (earlier in July) provided brief support; otherwise the stock continues to drift lower with broader semiconductor weakness acting as an additional headwind.

### Key Risk Status

| Risk | Status |
|---|---|
| **Marvell/Celestial AI order cancellation & NDA lawsuit** | **Active.** Marvell canceled all POs April 23, 2026, citing NDA breach (POET exec discussed confidential Celestial AI shipping details in public YouTube interview on April 21). Stock dropped 47.3% on April 27 disclosure. NDA dispute unresolved — no settlement announced. |
| **Securities class actions (PFIC tax misrepresentation)** | **Active / post-deadline.** Lead plaintiff deadline passed June 29, 2026. Multiple law firms (Rosen, Kirby McInerney, Faruqi & Faruqi, Block & Leviton) have active suits. Core allegation: POET concealed likely PFIC status under U.S. tax law while simultaneously breaching customer NDA. Litigation risk remains material. |
| **$400M raise dilution impact** | **Dilution locked in.** Closed May 18, 2026: 19.05M shares at $21.00/unit + warrants for 19.05M more shares (exercisable at $26.25). Stock dropped 22% on close date. Total equity raised ~$830M over past year. Cash on hand ~$375M vs. Q4 2025 net loss of ~$43M/quarter run rate. Runway ample but per-share value materially impaired. |

### Outlook: **BEARISH (near-term)**
Multiple legal overhangs with no near-term resolution, stock down ~50% from highs on a combination of customer loss + NDA breach + dilution + litigation. The 1M units/month optical engine target for end-2027 is the long-term bull case, but the path there requires re-establishing customer trust and stabilizing the capital structure — neither of which is imminent. Any near-term bounces are likely short-covering, not fundamental inflection.
