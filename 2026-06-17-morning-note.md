## Macro Backdrop

US equity futures are edging modestly higher overnight (S&P +0.15%, Nasdaq +0.21%, Dow +0.12%) after a bifurcated Monday session where mega-cap tech sold off hard — Nvidia -2.4%, Broadcom -4.4%, Micron -6.2%, AMD -7.3%, Intel -8.5% — while the Dow closed +0.64% on cyclical strength. The dominant theme today is the first Federal Open Market Committee meeting under new Fed Chair Kevin Warsh. Markets widely expect a hold (funds rate 3.50–3.75%), but May CPI printing at 4.2% YoY — a three-year high — has futures markets pricing a quarter-point hike as early as December. Oil is the other macro tailwind: WTI dropped below $80/bbl for the first time since March (-4%), with Brent falling a similar amount on growing optimism around a US-Iran ceasefire that could reopen the Strait of Hormuz. The G7 Summit wraps today in Evian-les-Bains, France, with coordination on Ukraine, energy security, and AI governance in focus.

## POET Technologies (POET) Daily Update

**Current price:** ~$13.93 | **Daily change:** +11.17%

The stock is bouncing sharply (+11%) on no specific new catalyst — likely short covering and retail speculative flow ahead of the June 26 shareholder meeting and June 29 class action deadline. Context is key: POET is still trading ~33% below its pre-collapse level (~$20.81 52-week high) and ~50% off its May offering price of $21.00.

### News in the Last 24 Hours
No material new filings or press releases identified for June 16–17, 2026. The most recent material 6-K disclosed POET's PFIC status for 2025 and its intention to redomicile to the US (shareholder vote scheduled June 26, 2026). Q1 2026 revenue was $503,389 — negligible, underscoring that the investment thesis is entirely forward-looking.

### Key Risk Status

**(1) Marvell/Celestial AI order cancellation & NDA lawsuit:** Marvell (which acquired Celestial AI) canceled all purchase orders on April 27, 2026, citing POET CFO's public social media disclosure of confidential order and shipping details on April 21, 2026. Stock fell 47.3% on the announcement. The NDA breach opens POET to counterparty legal liability in addition to the lost revenue; no resolution or settlement announced. **Status: unresolved, material downside risk.**

**(2) Securities class actions (PFIC tax misrepresentation):** Multiple firms (Rosen Law, Pomerantz, Faruqi & Faruqi, Block & Leviton) have filed or are investigating class actions covering the period April 1–27, 2026. Core allegation: POET misrepresented its likely PFIC status under US tax law (negative tax treatment for US shareholders), using equivocal language to downplay a near-certain classification. POET has now confirmed it expects PFIC status for 2025 and is seeking shareholder approval June 26 to redomicile to the US. **Lead plaintiff deadline: June 29, 2026. Status: active, approaching key date.**

**(3) $400M raise dilution impact:** POET closed a registered direct offering on May 18, 2026 at $21.00/share (combined share + warrant), issuing 19,047,620 new shares plus warrants exercisable at $26.15 for an additional 19,047,620 shares. The raise triggered a 22% stock drop. At today's ~$13.93 price, both the offering price and the warrant strike are deeply out of the money. Full exercise of warrants would more than double the dilution impact. **Status: fully dilutive overhang intact; warrants unlikely to be exercised at current prices, which removes near-term secondary pressure but also signals market skepticism of the company's value.**

### Outlook: **BEARISH / SPECULATIVE**
Today's +11% move is not fundamentally driven — it's noise in a high-volatility stock with multiple unresolved legal overhangs, a lost anchor customer, and revenue of under $1M/quarter against a $2.4B market cap. The June 26 redomicile vote and June 29 class action deadline are binary catalysts. Avoid new long exposure ahead of these dates. Bulls would need to see a new commercial anchor customer or legal settlement; neither appears imminent.
